Not all utility effects require conscious awareness activation. Some are
automatically activated through SYS 0 or SYS 1 processes. This section
establishes the critical \textbf{Instant Exception Rule}.

\subsubsection{Instant INU: Axiom AWX-A35}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{axiom}[AWX-A35: Instant INU]
\label{ax:instant-inu}
Physiologically immediate individual utility effects do not require awareness 
activation---they occur automatically.
\end{axiom}

\paragraph{Formal Condition.}
\begin{equation}
\text{If } U_{pot,INU} \in \{\text{physiologically immediate}\} \quad \Rightarrow \quad A_{INU}(t^*) = 1
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Examples of Instant INU.}
\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Instant INU Examples}
\label{tab:instant-inu-examples}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Stimulus} & \textbf{Utility Effect} & \textbf{FEPSDE Dimension} & \textbf{Time} \\
\midrule
Pain & $U^{INU}_{P}$ threat & Physical & Milliseconds \\
Hunger & $U^{INU}_{P}$ need & Physical & Seconds \\
Fatigue & $U^{INU}_{P}$ depletion & Physical & Seconds \\
Dopamine hit & $U^{INU}_{E}$ pleasure & Emotional & Milliseconds \\
Sexual arousal & $U^{INU}_{E}$ drive & Emotional & Seconds \\
Startle/fear & $U^{INU}_{E}$ threat & Emotional & Milliseconds \\
Taste pleasure & $U^{INU}_{E}$ reward & Emotional & Milliseconds \\
\bottomrule

\end{table}

\paragraph{Mechanism.}
These effects bypass cognitive processing entirely. The body ``knows''
before the mind reflects. This is evolutionarily adaptive---waiting
for cognitive processing before responding to pain or danger would be fatal.

%------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection{No Instant KNU: Axiom AWX-A37}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{axiom}[AWX-A37: No Instant KNU]
\label{ax:no-instant-knu}
Collective utility effects are \textbf{never} instant. All KNU effects 
require deliberate awareness activation.
\end{axiom}

\paragraph{Formal Condition.}
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\forall U_{pot,KNU}: \quad A_{KNU}(t^*) < 1 \text{ without explicit awareness activation}}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Why KNU Cannot Be Instant.}
Collective welfare is inherently abstract. It cannot be:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Felt in the body} (unlike INU pain, hunger, pleasure)
\item \textbf{Experienced through immediate social-emotional reaction} (unlike IDN shame, pride)
\item \textbf{Perceived without cognitive mediation} (requires understanding of collective)
\end{itemize}

Aggregation & Individual unit & Requires summing \\

\paragraph{The Deepest Asymmetry.}
The no-instant-KNU rule creates the \emph{deepest} awareness asymmetry:

\begin{equation}
A^{self}_{INU,instant} = 1 \quad \text{vs.} \quad A^{comm}_{KNU} = \text{requires SYS 2}
\end{equation}

This is why collective action problems are so persistent across cultures
and time periods---they are not cultural or institutional failures but
structural consequences of how awareness operates.

%------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsubsection{Mediated Instant KNU: The Indirect Path}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

While \emph{direct} instant KNU is impossible, KNU can become 
\emph{indirectly} instant through IDN or empathy:

\begin{equation}
A_{KNU,indirect} = f(A_{IDN,instant}) \quad \text{or} \quad f(Empathy_{immediate})
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Path 1: Through Identity.}
If collective welfare is part of personal identity, it can activate instantly:

\begin{itemize}
\item ``I am an environmentalist'' → $A^{self}_{IDN}$ triggers $A^{self}_{KNU}$
\item ``I am a team player'' → $A^{self}_{IDN}$ triggers $A^{self}_{KNU}$
\item ``I am a patriot'' → $A^{self}_{IDN}$ triggers $A^{self}_{KNU}$
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Path 2: Through Empathy.}
If others' welfare is emotionally felt (SYS 1), it can approach instant:

\begin{itemize}
\item Parent sees child in danger → Immediate response
\item Witnessing suffering → Empathic distress
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Example: The Parent's Dilemma.}
A parent sees their child in danger:
\begin{itemize}
\item Direct: $U^{KNU}_{family}$ (family welfare) --- not instant
\item Mediated: $U^{IDN}_{parent}$ (parental identity) triggers instant response
\end{itemize}

The parent acts ``for the group'' but through identity, not through direct
collective utility awareness.

\subsubsection{Summary: The Instant Exception Hierarchy}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=blue!5!white, colframe=blue!75!black, title=The Instant Exception Rule]

\textbf{Three Categories, Three Rules:}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{INU Instant (AWX-A35):} Physiologically immediate effects 
$\Rightarrow A = 1$ automatic
\item \textbf{IDN Instant (AWX-A36):} Socially-emotionally immediate effects 
$\Rightarrow A = 1$ automatic
\item \textbf{KNU Never Instant (AWX-A37):} Collective effects 
$\Rightarrow A < 1$ always requires activation
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{The Critical Implication:}
$$A^{self}_{INU,instant} = 1 \quad \text{vs.} \quad A^{comm}_{KNU} \approx 0 \text{ (without deliberate effort)}$$

This structural asymmetry explains why self-interest systematically 
dominates collective welfare in the absence of intervention.

\end{tcolorbox}
