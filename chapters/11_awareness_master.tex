\section{Introduction: From Potential to Effective Utility}
\label{sec:awareness-intro}

%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Chapter~\ref{ch:welfare-fepsde} established what \emph{could} generate utility:
144 components across three categories (INU, KNU, IDN), six dimensions (FEPSDE),
four time horizons, and two valences. But this represents \emph{potential} utility.
In any actual decision moment, only a subset of this potential becomes effective.

\begin{intuition}[Why Awareness Matters]
Consider Anna choosing between a promotion (+30\% salary, +15 hours/week) and her current job:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Potential utility:} Health impact ($U^P = -0.25$), family time ($U^S = -0.40$), identity ($IDN = +0.15$)
  \item \textbf{At decision moment:} Anna focuses only on salary ($U^F = +0.30$), with $A(U^F) = 0.95$
  \item \textbf{Result:} $A(U^{P,S,IDN}) \approx 0.05$ --- these dimensions barely enter her calculus
\end{itemize}
Six months later, experiencing burnout and family strain, she realizes what she missed.
Net utility: $0.30 \times 0.95 - 0.25 - 0.40 + 0.15 = -0.22$ (negative!).
The utility was always there---she just wasn't \textbf{aware} of it when deciding.
\end{intuition}

\begin{center}
\fcolorbox{orange!75!black}{orange!5!white}{\parbox{0.85\textwidth}{

}}

\paragraph{The Transformation Condition.}
The Awareness Function $A(\cdot)$ is a \emph{necessary condition} for utility to
influence behavior. Without awareness, even high potential utility has no effect:

\begin{definition}[The Transformation Principle]
\begin{equation}
U_{eff,i,d,t} = A_{i,d,t}(t^*) \times U_{pot,i,d,t}
\label{eq:transformation-principle}
\end{equation}
where $A_{i,d,t}(t^*) \in [0,1]$ is the awareness of utility component $(i,d,t)$
at the Moment of Truth $t^*$.
\end{definition}
