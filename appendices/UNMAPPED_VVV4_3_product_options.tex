\subsection{Why This Matters}

The EBF framework provides powerful theoretical tools for understanding economic and social behavior. However, theoretical elegance alone does not create practical impact. \section{Worked Example}
\label{sec:vvv3-example}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Example: Enterprise Product Selection}

\paragraph{Setup.} A large consulting firm wants to license EBF products to enhance their service offerings. They have limited budget for initial integration and need to select 2-3 products to start.

\paragraph{Application.} Using the Product Portfolio Prioritization:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Assess client needs}: Primary clients are corporations seeking ESG reporting and HR transformation
\item \textbf{Match to products}:
    \begin{itemize}
    \item ESG reporting $\to$ Stakeholder Impact Analyzer (WHO)
    \item HR transformation $\to$ FEPSDE Diagnostic Suite (WHAT)
    \item Both benefit from $\to$ Context Detection Engine (WHEN)
    \end{itemize}
\item \textbf{Consider complexity}: Start with FEPSDE (Low complexity) before Stakeholder Analyzer (High complexity)
\item \textbf{Revenue model fit}: Consulting firm prefers per-engagement pricing $\to$ FEPSDE (\$5-50/assessment) fits well
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Result.} Recommended sequence:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Phase 1: FEPSDE Diagnostic Suite (immediate, low complexity)
\item Phase 2: Stakeholder Impact Analyzer (after 6 months)
\item Phase 3: Context Detection Engine (after 12 months)
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Discussion.} The selection prioritizes quick wins (FEPSDE) while building toward higher-value products. The phased approach manages integration risk and allows capability building.

\section{Critical Foundations and Objections}
\label{sec:vvv3-foundations}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%

\subsection{Objection 1: Academic Framework Cannot Be Productized}

\textbf{Objection:} Academic frameworks are too abstract and complex to translate into practical products that customers will pay for.

\textbf{Response:} The 10C CORE structure was designed with productization in mind. Each CORE answers a specific, actionable question (WHO, WHAT, HOW, WHEN, WHERE, AWARE, READY) that maps directly to business needs. The success of products like personality assessments (Big Five $\to$ commercial tools) and decision support systems demonstrates that behavioral science can be successfully commercialized.

\subsection{Objection 2: Market Size Estimates Are Speculative}

\textbf{Objection:} The revenue projections and market size estimates have no empirical basis.

\textbf{Response:} Acknowledged. Market estimates should be validated through customer discovery and pilot programs before major investment. The prioritization framework remains valid even if absolute numbers shift---relative comparisons (Context Detection larger than Awareness Assessment) are robust.
