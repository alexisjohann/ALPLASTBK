# Executive Summary

UBS plans to introduce a cryptocurrency investment offering, initially limited to two assets: Bitcoin and one additional cryptocurrency. The primary objective is customer retention – enabling existing investment clients to access crypto investments through UBS rather than seeking alternatives with competitors who already offer such products.

The strategic challenge is communication, not innovation: How can UBS communicate the crypto offering in a way that resonates with existing investment clients, aligns with UBS's brand identity (trust, stability, expertise), and addresses the identity drivers of relevant customer segments – without positioning UBS as a disruptive innovator or undermining its core brand values?

**The core challenge:** UBS must communicate a product category often associated with innovation, disruption, and risk in a manner consistent with its positioning around trust, security, and expertise. The communication must activate relevant segments while explicitly avoiding "innovation" framing that could create cognitive dissonance with UBS's brand identity.

This engagement follows a two-stage approach: First, a comprehensive analysis of UBS's investment client segments and their crypto-related attitudes, followed by the development of macro-level communication strategy and micro-level communication patterns tailored to prioritised segments.

# Question 1: Segment Analysis and Crypto Affinity

## The Strategic Question

How do UBS's investment client segments differ in their characteristics, behavioural profiles, and attitudes toward cryptocurrency – and what are the specific barriers and drivers for crypto adoption within each segment?

## Part A: Segment Profiling for Investment Behaviour

The consultant must develop comprehensive profiles of all five segments with respect to their general investment behaviour:

**Behavioural Characteristics**
- What are the dominant decision-making patterns within each segment?
- How do segments differ in information search, evaluation, and decision processes?
- What role does the advisor relationship play in investment decisions?
- What are the primary investment motivations (growth, security, status, independence)?

**Sociodemographic Analysis**
- What are the demographic distributions within each segment (age, income, wealth, education)?
- How do segments differ in regional distribution and urban/rural patterns?
- What life stage characteristics define each segment?
- What professional backgrounds are typical?

**Identity and Values**
- What self-image and identity narratives characterise each segment?
- What values drive investment decisions (security, autonomy, achievement, belonging)?
- How does the UBS relationship fit into the customer's identity?
- What are the "jobs to be done" that investing fulfills for each segment?

## Part B: Crypto-Specific Attitudes and Behaviours

For each segment, the consultant must analyse crypto-specific factors:

**Current Crypto Behaviour**
- What proportion of each segment has existing crypto exposure (through other providers)?
- What is the level of crypto knowledge and literacy within each segment?
- What information sources do different segments use for crypto information?
- What past experiences (positive or negative) shape segment attitudes?

**Crypto Attitudes and Perceptions**
- How does each segment perceive crypto as an asset class (speculation vs. investment)?
- What emotional associations does crypto evoke (excitement, fear, scepticism, curiosity)?
- How does crypto fit (or conflict) with segment identity and self-image?
- What narratives about crypto resonate with or repel each segment?

**Barriers and Drivers**
- What are the primary barriers to crypto adoption for each segment?
- Which barriers are cognitive (complexity, understanding) vs. emotional (fear, distrust)?
- What would motivate each segment to consider crypto investment?
- What role does "UBS offering crypto" play in legitimising or normalising the asset class?

## Part C: Identity-Crypto Alignment Analysis

The consultant must specifically analyse the identity dynamics at play:

**Identity Consistency**
- For which segments is crypto investment consistent with their investor identity?
- Where does crypto create identity conflict or cognitive dissonance?
- How can UBS help segments reconcile crypto with their existing self-concept?

**Social Dimensions**
- What social pressures (pro or con) affect crypto attitudes in each segment?
- How do peer behaviours influence segment openness to crypto?
- What signalling value does crypto investment have within each segment's social context?

## Deliverable Requirements

1. Comprehensive segment profiles covering demographics, behaviours, and investment patterns
2. Crypto attitude mapping for each segment (knowledge, interest, barriers, drivers)
3. Identity-crypto alignment analysis identifying consistency and conflict points
4. Barrier taxonomy distinguishing cognitive, emotional, and social barriers by segment
5. Current crypto behaviour assessment (existing exposure, information sources, experiences)
6. Segment-specific opportunity sizing (addressable market by segment)

# Question 2: Segment Prioritisation for UBS

## The Strategic Question

Which segments should UBS prioritise for crypto communication, which segments should be approached later, and which segments should explicitly not be targeted – and what is the rationale based on strategic fit, retention risk, and activation potential?

## Part A: Prioritisation Framework Development

The consultant must develop a multi-criteria prioritisation framework:

**Retention Risk Assessment**
- Which segments are most likely to defect to competitors for crypto access?
- What is the revenue and relationship value at risk by segment?
- How advanced are defection signals within each segment?
- What is the time sensitivity of retention intervention?

**Activation Potential Assessment**
- What is the realistic conversion potential within each segment?
- How much effort (communication, support) is required to activate each segment?
- What is the expected ROI of activation efforts by segment?
- How does segment size affect total addressable opportunity?

**Strategic Fit Assessment**
- How well does crypto communication to each segment align with UBS brand?
- Which segments can be addressed without brand risk?
- Where does crypto communication strengthen vs. weaken brand positioning?
- How does segment prioritisation affect overall market perception?

## Part B: Segment Ranking and Rationale

Based on the framework, the consultant must deliver:

**Priority Segments**
- Which 1-2 segments should receive primary focus?
- What makes these segments the optimal starting point?
- What is the expected impact of prioritising these segments?

**Secondary Segments**
- Which segments should be addressed in a second phase?
- What conditions or learnings should trigger secondary segment activation?
- How should secondary segment communication differ from priority segments?

**Anti-Personas**
- Which segments should explicitly NOT be targeted?
- What are the risks of communicating crypto to these segments?
- How should UBS handle inquiries from non-target segments?

## Part C: Competitive and Market Context

The prioritisation must consider market dynamics:

**Competitive Landscape**
- Which competitors are successfully attracting UBS clients with crypto offerings?
- What positioning and communication approaches are competitors using?
- Where is competitive pressure most acute by segment?

**Market Timing**
- How does current crypto market sentiment affect segment prioritisation?
- What market conditions would accelerate or decelerate prioritisation?
- How should prioritisation adapt to market volatility?

## Deliverable Requirements

7. Multi-criteria prioritisation framework with weighted scoring methodology
8. Segment ranking with detailed rationale for each priority level
9. Anti-persona profiles specifying which segments to avoid and why
10. Competitive analysis mapping competitor positioning by segment
11. Timing recommendations including sequencing and trigger conditions
12. Risk assessment for prioritisation decisions

# Question 3: Communication Strategy (Macro Level)

## The Strategic Question

What overarching communication strategy will enable UBS to position its crypto offering in a way that is consistent with UBS brand values, resonates with target segments, and effectively transfers UBS's trust equity to the crypto product?

## Part A: Positioning Framework

The consultant must develop a positioning that navigates the brand-crypto tension:

**The Core Challenge**
Cryptocurrency is commonly associated with innovation, disruption, speculation, and risk. UBS is positioned around trust, stability, expertise, and security. The communication strategy must bridge this gap without compromising either the crypto opportunity or the UBS brand.

**Positioning Options Analysis**
The consultant must evaluate positioning approaches:

| Positioning Approach | Description | Brand Fit | Crypto Fit |
|:---------------------|:------------|:----------|:-----------|
| **Access Provider** | "UBS gives you access to crypto" | High | High |
| **Security Wrapper** | "Crypto, made secure" | High | Medium |
| **Expertise Guide** | "Navigate crypto with UBS expertise" | High | High |
| **Modernisation** | "UBS evolves with the market" | Medium | High |
| **Innovation Leader** | "UBS leads in digital assets" | Low | High |

**Recommended Positioning Direction**
- What positioning best serves retention objectives?
- How should the positioning differ between segments (if at all)?
- What positioning risks must be managed?

## Part B: Brand Fit and Trust Transfer

**Brand Consistency Analysis**
- Which elements of UBS brand identity support crypto communication?
- Which brand elements create tension with crypto?
- How should communication acknowledge and manage this tension?

**Trust Transfer Mechanisms**
- How can UBS's accumulated trust equity be transferred to the crypto offering?
- What proof points demonstrate UBS's capability to offer crypto safely?
- What reassurance elements are needed to maintain trust during crypto adoption?

**Brand Protection Guidelines**
- What communication approaches could damage UBS brand?
- What language, imagery, and tone should be avoided?
- How should crypto be framed to protect UBS's position with non-crypto segments?

## Part C: Messaging Architecture

The consultant must develop a structured messaging framework:

**Core Message**
- What single, compelling statement captures UBS crypto value proposition?
- How does this message differ from competitor positioning?
- How does the core message connect to UBS brand essence?

**Supporting Messages**
- What supporting messages address specific segment concerns?
- What proof points and evidence support the core message?
- How should messages be sequenced for maximum impact?

**Risk Communication**
- How should crypto risks be communicated in a UBS-appropriate manner?
- What is the right balance of transparency and reassurance?
- How does risk communication differ from competitors?

**Tonality Guidelines**
- What tone of voice is appropriate for UBS crypto communication?
- What language choices align with UBS positioning (avoid: "revolutionary", "disruptive")?
- How should tonality differ across channels and segments?

## Deliverable Requirements

13. Positioning framework with recommended approach and rationale
14. Brand fit analysis mapping UBS brand elements to crypto communication
15. Trust transfer mechanism design with specific proof points and reassurance elements
16. Messaging architecture including core message, supporting messages, and proof points
17. Risk communication guidelines appropriate to UBS positioning
18. Tonality and language guidelines specifying recommended and prohibited terminology

# Question 4: Communication Patterns (Micro Level)

## The Strategic Question

What specific communication patterns, tactics, and interventions are most effective for activating prioritised segments – including channel strategies, trigger moments, advisor enablement, and objection handling?

## Part A: Segment-Specific Communication Design

For each prioritised segment, the consultant must develop tailored communication approaches:

**Message Customisation**
- How should the core message be adapted for each segment?
- What segment-specific benefits should be emphasised?
- What concerns must be proactively addressed by segment?

**Framing Strategies**
- What psychological frames are most effective for each segment?
- Should communication emphasise opportunity (gain frame) or risk of missing out (loss frame)?
- How should complexity and simplicity be balanced by segment?

**Visual and Content Guidelines**
- What visual approaches resonate with each segment?
- What content formats are most effective (detailed analysis, simple summaries, comparisons)?
- What social proof elements work for each segment?

## Part B: Channel and Touchpoint Strategy

**Channel Selection**
- Which channels are most effective for each segment (advisor, digital, direct mail, branch)?
- What is the optimal channel mix by segment?
- How do channels complement each other in the customer journey?

**Advisor Role and Enablement**
- What role should client advisors play in crypto communication?
- How should advisors be equipped to handle crypto conversations?
- What support materials do advisors need?
- How should advisors handle segments they should not actively target?

**Digital Channel Strategy**
- What digital touchpoints should feature crypto communication?
- How should crypto be integrated into digital banking experience?
- What self-service information and tools should be available?

## Part C: Trigger Moments and Activation

**Trigger Identification**
- What events or moments create openness to crypto communication?
- How can UBS identify and respond to individual trigger moments?
- What external triggers (market events, media coverage) should prompt communication?

**Activation Sequences**
- What is the optimal sequence of communications to drive activation?
- How should communication intensity escalate or de-escalate?
- What follow-up sequences support conversion?

**Objection Handling**
- What are the most common objections by segment?
- What responses effectively address each objection?
- How should advisors and digital channels handle objections differently?

## Part D: Social Proof and Peer Effects

**Social Proof Strategies**
- What social proof elements are most credible for each segment?
- How can peer adoption be communicated without appearing manipulative?
- What testimonial or case study approaches are appropriate?

**Peer Effect Activation**
- How can satisfied early adopters influence their peers?
- What referral or recommendation mechanisms could be effective?
- How does social dynamics differ between segments?

## Deliverable Requirements

19. Segment-specific messaging with customised value propositions and benefit emphasis
20. Channel strategy with recommended mix and sequencing by segment
21. Advisor enablement concept including conversation guides and support materials
22. Trigger moment catalogue with recommended responses and activation sequences
23. Objection handling guide with segment-specific responses
24. Social proof strategy with credibility-appropriate approaches by segment
25. Do's and Don'ts communication guide with concrete examples

# Integration Across the Four Questions

The four strategic questions form an integrated analytical framework:

**Question 1** (segment analysis) provides the foundational understanding of who UBS's investment clients are, how they differ in crypto attitudes, and what barriers and drivers exist. This foundation informs all subsequent decisions.

**Question 2** (segment prioritisation) translates segment understanding into strategic focus. The prioritisation determines where communication resources should be concentrated and which segments require different approaches or should be avoided.

**Question 3** (macro communication strategy) establishes the overarching framework that ensures all communication is consistent with UBS brand values and effectively transfers trust to the crypto offering. This strategy constrains and guides micro-level tactics.

**Question 4** (micro communication patterns) operationalises the strategy into specific, actionable communication tactics tailored to prioritised segments. These patterns must be consistent with the macro strategy while addressing segment-specific needs.

The final communication strategy must synthesise insights from all four questions into a coherent, actionable recommendation that balances retention urgency, brand protection, and activation effectiveness.

# BEATRIX Integration

This engagement leverages the BEATRIX framework for systematic behavioural analysis:

## Behavioural Economics Foundations

BEATRIX applies behavioural economics principles central to this engagement:

**Identity and Self-Concept**
- Investment decisions are deeply connected to investor identity
- Crypto adoption requires identity consistency or identity expansion
- Communication must help segments integrate crypto into their self-concept

**Trust and Credibility**
- Trust is a key asset that can be transferred to new offerings
- Trust transfer requires specific proof points and reassurance mechanisms
- Crypto communication must protect UBS's trust position

**Loss Aversion and Risk Perception**
- Crypto is perceived as risky by most traditional investors
- Loss aversion shapes how risks should be communicated
- Framing significantly affects risk perception

**Social Proof and Norms**
- Peer behaviour strongly influences investment decisions
- Social proof must be credible and segment-appropriate
- Normalisation of crypto reduces adoption barriers

## Context Analysis (Ψ-Framework)

The BCM 2.0 Ψ-context dimensions provide systematic analysis of:

- **Ψ_I (Institutional):** UBS brand constraints, regulatory environment, compliance requirements
- **Ψ_S (Social):** Peer effects, social norms around crypto, advisor relationships
- **Ψ_C (Cognitive):** Crypto knowledge, complexity barriers, decision-making patterns
- **Ψ_K (Cultural):** Swiss values, conservative vs. progressive attitudes, generational differences
- **Ψ_E (Economic):** Risk budgets, portfolio allocation, competitive pricing
- **Ψ_T (Temporal):** Market timing, decision urgency, adoption curves

## Complementarity Analysis

The framework analyses critical interactions:

- UBS brand identity and crypto category associations
- Retention urgency and brand protection requirements
- Segment activation potential and resource requirements
- Advisor enablement and scalable digital communication
