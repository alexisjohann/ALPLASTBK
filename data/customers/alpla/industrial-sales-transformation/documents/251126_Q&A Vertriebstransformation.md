Q&A – Market Potentials at ALPLA Industrial
1. Why is the sales organization changing at all?
In recent years, ALPLA Industrial has grown significantly – with dedicated teams in multiple countries and strong customer relationships.At 
the
 same time, 
it
 
has
 
become
 
clear
 
that
:
We often work side by side rather than together.
Knowledge remains local, and market potential goes untapped.
Customers don’t always experience us as one unified partner.
This transformation creates the structure to better connect our efforts – for faster coordination, stronger impact, and one clear voice to the market.

The change is not about replacing people, it’s about how we collaborate, across plants and countries, with shared clarity and purpose.
3. What does this mean for my role in Sales or Customer Service?
The new structure will bring more clarity and orientation:
Who owns which customer relationship?
What are the rules for pricing, offers, and escalation?
How do we share insights and feedback quickly?
For many of you, daily work will become more streamlined and transparent.There are also new opportunities, especially for those in Customer Service Lead roles, Sales Managers, or Key Account Management (KAM).
4. Is the new structure already fixed?
Yes – the future model is confirmed:
A centrally led, segment-driven sales structure with local anchoring.
Governance principles will be introduced starting Q1 2026.
However: The details of implementation are still open, your input, feedback and experience matter. This

6. What about current responsibilities (e.g. Plant Managers)?
Plant managers do not lose relevance, they gain more focus on their operational core responsibilities.Sales coordination and customer communication will be streamlined centrally, allowing local operations to run more efficiently and reliably.
7. Who can I reach out to if I have questions?
Feel free to contact Roland Baumann or Thomas Maibohm at any time. Your site meetings are also designed to address any open questions you may have.
