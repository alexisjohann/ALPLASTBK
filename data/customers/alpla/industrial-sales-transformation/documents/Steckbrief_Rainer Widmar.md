Interview Takeaways – Steckbrief
General Information
  Name: Rainer Widmar
  Role: Managing Director, Region Zentral- und Osteuropa (ALPLA Group)
  Date of Interview: 29. Oktober 2025
  Conducted by: FehrAdvice & Partners AG
Key Points Discussed
Stream 1 – Positioning & Services
Goal: Market identity, customer value, differentiation potential
Key Statements
ALPLA Industrial = B2B specialist for buckets and canisters; long-standing technical expertise.
Positioned as reliable partner; rebranding from Wolf Plastics to ALPLA Industrial 1.5–2 years ago – smoother recognition externally, though internally still a transition.
Brand alignment strengthens visibility under the ALPLA umbrella (as with ALPLA Recycling, Pharma etc.).
Core business historically Tier-2 / Tier-3 customers in construction; Tier-1 Key Accounts underdeveloped.
Greatest growth potential = large Key Accounts outside construction, e.g. food industry.
Geographic potential: Poland and unserved regions.
Customers perceive ALPLA Industrial as reliable, flexible, quality-focused, now with stronger sustainability credentials (certifications, recycled material access).
Differentiation levers: sustainability, recycling expertise, process reliability, digital printing capability.
Digital Printing example: regulatory change → labels on plastic soon banned → inline printing = new value field.
ALPLA Industrial lacks in-house design competence (depends on external know-how or ALPLA Packaging PDC).
Interpretation→ Widmar frames ALPLA Industrial as a trusted, technically solid but under-scaled brand.→ He sees strategic differentiation through sustainability and services, not through product diversity.→ Biggest opportunity = move up-market to Tier-1 Key Accounts by transferring ALPLA’s packaging-world capabilities.
Implications
Build a formal Key Account Management model inside ALPLA Industrial.
Integrate Design for Recycling and Digital Printing into the value proposition.
Position ALPLA Industrial as “reliable + innovative partner” for large B2B clients.
Stream 2 – Automation & Efficiency
Goal: Systems, tools, digital capability
Key Statements
ALPLA Industrial not yet fully integrated into ALPLA Group IT systems.
Legacy (Wolf Plastics) tools still operate; resources and investment limit full integration.
No CRM yet – group-wide roll-out pending.
Product catalogue exists but poor usability and unclear contact process.
No webshop – digital visibility low.
Digital transformation “in progress.”
Interpretation→ Digital infrastructure lags behind ALPLA standards.→ Customer-facing digital channels missing (despite growing importance for buyers).
Implications
Prioritise CRM implementation and catalogue revamp.
Develop web-based sales tools and enhance digital reach.
Align Industrial’s systems with Group for synergy and transparency.
Stream 3 – Organisation & Steering
Goal: Collaboration, governance, cross-plant integration
Key Statements
Three plants (AT, HU, RO); cooperation Sales ↔ plants 8/10, HQ support 8/10.
Variability by personality: Romania 8–9, Hungary 4–5.
Between plants = 4/10 – limited exchange on capacity, markets, products.
Missing structured alignment and cross-selling processes.
Matrix creates healthy goal tension between HQ and plants – manageable if incentives aligned.
Overlap with ALPLA Packaging customers = opportunity if synergies are actively managed.
Need for more Key Account skills and dedicated roles (not plant GMs acting as KAMs).
Interpretation→ Systemic collaboration exists but relies on individual goodwill.→ Leadership sees integration as the next frontier: one regional sales architecture for CEE.
Implications
Establish cross-plant sales governance and regular capacity/market exchange.
Define Key Account ownership and reporting lines.
Align targets and KPIs to avoid plant vs group conflicts.
Stream 4 – Incentive & Motivation
Goal: Performance logic and reward alignment
Key Statements
Incentive systems must be coherent and non-contradictory across matrix levels.
Current setup: annual bonuses, no monthly or quarterly schemes.
Widmar skeptical of “American-style commission systems.”
However, for standard products, short-term variable systems could make sense (e.g., if seller promotes similar product for faster sale).
Key management task: design incentives that link individual motivation to group targets.
Interpretation→ Prefers stability and alignment over aggressive individual incentives.→ Acknowledges need for flexibility in commodity sales contexts.
Implications
Review bonus architecture to ensure group/plant consistency.
Consider dual model (annual + transactional elements) for Industrial.
Keep cultural fit and clarity paramount in design.
