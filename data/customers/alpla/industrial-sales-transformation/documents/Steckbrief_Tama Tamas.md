Interview Takeaways – Steckbrief
General Information
  Name: Tamas Tama
  Role: General Manager Hungary (ALPLA Industrial)
  Date of Interview: 29. Oktober 2025
  Conducted by: FehrAdvice & Partners AG
Key Points Discussed
Stream 1 – Positioning & Services
Goal: Market understanding, customer value, differentiation
Key Statements
Sales structure focuses on long-standing industrial customers (construction materials: Baumit, PPG, Saint-Gobain Weber).
Hungary handles Central & Eastern Europe (Hungary, Slovakia, Czech Rep.), while Austria covers Western Europe (Germany, Switzerland, France).
Customers in the region are loyal but highly price-sensitive and give no long-term commitments.
No co-development or design work: customers ask for existing shapes only; if ALPLA Industrial doesn’t have it, they buy elsewhere.
ALPLA Industrial is not the cheapest supplier but wins through speed, responsiveness, reliability, and personal contact.
Customers appreciate quick replies and problem-solving; many switch from competitors due to slow response.
Physical presence and visiting customers are central to the Hungarian sales model.
Around 260 active customers; focus on maintaining relationships and expanding regionally.
Food packaging seen as a potential new segment, identified through fairs and retail scouting.
Interpretation→ The Hungarian organisation positions ALPLA Industrial as a reliable partner rather than a low-cost player.→ Differentiation comes from service quality and responsiveness, not product innovation.→ Market growth depends on field intensity and relationship building, not portfolio expansion.
Implications
Reinforce the narrative “reliable and responsive partner” across markets.
Support Hungarian team with marketing and digital visibility tools to scale personal reputation.
Re-evaluate product portfolio gaps (lack of co-development offers) for new sectors like food.
Stream 2 – Automation & Efficiency
Goal: Digital tools, processes, data integration
Key Statements
Salesforce recently introduced; used mainly for order management, not yet as CRM.
Integration with ERP: orders → delivery notes → invoices.
All customers are registered; process flow largely automated, but no analytical or customer-development use yet.
No visibility into how other plants (Austria, Romania) manage their systems.
No need for additional external support, but values simple, localised tools.
Market differences make centralised systems less relevant in his view.
Interpretation→ Digitalisation progressing, but limited to transactional automation.→ CRM potential underused; local teams prioritise operational flow over data analysis.→ Independence from other entities keeps processes efficient but fragmented.
Implications
Extend Salesforce usage toward true CRM and customer insight tracking.
Share digital best practices across plants to harmonise customer view.
Preserve local agility but align data standards with Group.
Stream 3 – Organisation & Steering
Goal: Collaboration, governance, coordination
Key Statements
Team setup: GM (Tama) + 2 salespersons + 4 customer-service staff.
GM personally manages key accounts; team covers mid-size and small clients.
Austrian sales team handles Western Europe; coordination exists but informal.
Key accounts: Baumit (HQ Austria – Oliver Baumgartner), PPG (France), Saint-Gobain Weber (Austria & CEE).
Regular contact routines:
Daily operational contact via customer service and sales.
Weekly internal sales meetings (Hungary/Slovakia/Czech).
Quarterly MS Teams meetings with PPG HQ (Paris) – joined by Romanian GM Dan Stere.
Annual physical HQ visits.
Daily collaboration across plants (Hungary, Romania, Austria) for common customers.
Sees value in local autonomy and adaptation to national specifics.
Requests more proactive sales activity from Austrian side; 2026 budget = zero new sales expected from Western Europe.
Interpretation→ Cross-plant coordination works for key accounts but remains personality-driven.→ The Hungarian team displays strong ownership and customer intimacy but limited strategic alignment with central sales.→ Regional synergy potential underutilised.
Implications
Formalise key-account collaboration protocols between Austria, Hungary, Romania.
Introduce shared planning rhythm for multinational customers.
Clarify territorial responsibilities to reduce overlaps and gaps.
Stream 4 – Incentive & Motivation
Goal: Motivation logic, reward design, culture
Key Statements
Hungarian salespeople receive fixed salaries only, no variable bonuses.
Western Europe relies on external contracted partners working on commission.
Tama believes bonuses reduce well-being and team spirit; past systems created stress and discontent.
Motivation comes from personal attitude and responsibility, not monetary triggers.
Emphasises that structural constraints (capacity, product fit, pricing) make volume-based bonuses meaningless.
Interpretation→ Intrinsic motivation culture; financial incentives seen as counterproductive.→ Strong emphasis on stability and fairness over performance differentiation.→ Cultural divergence from HQ’s incentive logic.
Implications
Maintain intrinsic and relational motivation drivers; avoid introducing stress-inducing bonuses.
Consider non-financial recognition formats (visibility, autonomy, development).
Design any incentive concepts with local adaptability in mind.
