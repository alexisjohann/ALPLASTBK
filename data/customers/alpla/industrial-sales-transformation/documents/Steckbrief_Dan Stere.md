Interview Takeaways – Steckbrief
General Information
  Name: Dan Stere
  Role: General Manager Romania (ALPLA Industrial)
  Date of Interview: 29. Oktober 2025
  Conducted by: FehrAdvice & Partners AG
Key Points Discussed
Stream 1 – Positioning & Services
Goal: Market understanding, customer value, differentiation
Key Statements
ALPLA Industrial Romania is in transition to fully integrate into ALPLA Group processes.
Core business: construction industry (paint and plaster producers); ambition to diversify into food packaging to reduce dependency.
Main competitor: Tres/Greiner JV, holding a near-monopoly on yoghurt cups; strong local presence for ~20 years.
Customers in food packaging hesitate to shift volumes from Tres/Greiner out of fear of losing access or facing retaliation.
Competition from local injection molders and suppliers from Turkey, Serbia and Bulgaria; these markets enjoy lower costs and currency advantages.
Product portfolio gap: no sub-1 L formats (cups, small containers).
For construction, pails 1–30 L are standard; for food, 1–10 L.
Barrier to entry in food: tooling investment requires customer commitment – “chicken-and-egg” problem.
Pails seen as commodity; shape identical → competition purely on price.
Key success factors: delivery reliability, product quality, and trust.
Customer satisfaction survey (past 2 years) ≈ 3.8/5 average; low response rate.
New market opportunity: digital presence and faster response expected as younger buyers shift online.
Interpretation→ Strong operational reputation but weak innovation and service differentiation.→ Market entry barriers in food require strategic support from ALPLA Group.→ Positioning needs shift from price & reliability to trust & partnership + digital relevance.
Implications
Refine Value Proposition per segment (Construction vs. Food).
Enable entry into Food via joint investment logic or shared risk tooling model.
Strengthen digital visibility (online presence, speed, transparency).
Stream 2 – Automation & Efficiency
Goal: Processes, digital tools, data visibility
Key Statements
Legacy (Wolf Plastics) systems were manual and fragmented.
Since September 2025: rollout of ALPLA Prod ERP, improving KPIs and alignment with Group.
Only Romania among ex-Wolf plants uses ALPLA Prod due to differing technical setups.
Next planned step: CRM introduction.
Need for automated costing/profitability tools pulling data directly into offers.
Quotation lead time inconsistent – from 1 hour to 2 days – due to lack of automation.
Desire for “one system” visibility (profitability, lead times, quotes).
Digital competencies beyond systems: importance of digital marketing and online buyer interfaces.
Interpretation→ Romania is leading digital adoption within former Wolf Plastics sites.→ Efficiency gains visible but pricing and CRM integration still missing.→ Digitalisation viewed strategically – not only process, also market access.
Implications
Extend ALPLA Prod rollout learnings to other plants.
Prioritise CRM & automated costing as next digital pillars.
Invest in digital sales channels (web, data-driven customer contact).
Stream 3 – Organisation & Steering
Goal: Collaboration, structure, governance
Key Statements
Team composition: GM + Sales Manager + sales support + invoicing.
Plan to split customer responsibility by size once support matures.
GM and Sales Manager both maintain customer contact – lean, agile setup.
Cross-plant collaboration: exists but unstructured; works ad-hoc for common construction clients.
Decision-making remains local for construction and food customers; central coordination limited.
Collaboration with ALPLA Packaging occasional and opportunity-driven, not systematic.
Historical perception of Romania as “the poor sister” (less investment priority) – improving in last 2 years.
Cross-functional exchange (operations, finance, maintenance) now regular and appreciated.
Interpretation→ Local organisation small but functional; high agility.→ Still lacking structured integration and standardised collaboration across entities.→ Cultural recovery underway – growing equality within Group.
Implications
Formalise cross-plant governance for shared customers.
Define clear role allocation between Sales Manager / GM / HQ.
Maintain local agility while embedding into Group steering.
Stream 4 – Incentive & Motivation
Goal: Motivation logic, reward systems, retention
Key Statements
Current annual bonus “not so sharp.”
Prefers quarterly bonus cycles linked to concrete KPIs.
Bonus system follows ALPLA Group rules; KPIs derived locally.
Salary gaps vs. market identified – risk of turnover and talent loss.
Benchmarking studies recommended to adjust pay levels.
Turnover directly impacts efficiency and customer relationships.
Emphasis on fair, motivating, transparent system over purely financial incentives.
Interpretation→ Incentive structure too generic; lacks immediacy and local relevance.→ Recognition gap → potential retention risk.
Implications
Pilot quarterly KPI-based bonus at plant level.
Conduct market salary benchmarking.
Integrate recognition and feedback into incentive logic.
