## Abstract

Strategic decision making balances uniqueness and generality: managers must craft context-specific actions while abstracting from past cases to identify transferable patterns. Whether contextualizing or abstracting information improves decision performance remains an open question. We investigate this via a laboratory NK task framed either abstractly (factors A, B, C) or contextually (adapted Lemonade Stand). Pre-tests reveal that context improves immediate performance, but abstract framing benefits individuals with strong STEM skills and fosters higher learning rates over repeated rounds. Behavioral patterns show more early exploration in context and steeper explore-exploit shifts in abstraction. Our findings contribute to strategic decision making research.

## 1. Introduction

Strategic decisions are among the most consequential choices in organizations, and their importance demands exceptionally high decision quality. Yet such decisions are inherently idiosyncratic—context-specific, difficult to replicate, and sensitive to timing and local conditions.

**Core tension:** Strategy is fundamentally shaped by the tension between uniqueness and generality.

- **Context/Uniqueness:** Crafting distinct positions and tailored actions
- **Abstraction/Generality:** Generalizing from past cases, inferring causal patterns, transferring insights

**Research Question:** Holding information and task complexity constant, how does generalizing vs. contextualizing information affect strategic decision performance?

**Theoretical perspectives:**
- *Situated cognition* (Orlikowski, 2000): Contextualization improves understanding via rich environmental cues
- *Ecological rationality* (Gigerenzer, 2019): Heuristics adapted to specific environments
- *Heuristics-and-biases*: Contextualized information can induce systematic errors

## 2. Theory and Hypotheses

### 2.1 Wason Selection Task Foundation

The Wason selection task (1968) demonstrates that people perform poorly with abstract conditional rules (e.g., "If A, then B") but improve substantially when the same task is embedded in familiar context.

**Mechanisms:**
1. Prior knowledge activation (Griggs & Cox, 1982)
2. Pragmatic reasoning schemas (Cheng & Holyoak, 1985, 1989)
3. Reduced cognitive load via meaningful anchors

### 2.2 Hypotheses

**H1:** Ceteris paribus, managerial performance in a strategic decision-making task is higher when the task is presented in a contextualized rather than abstract form.

**H2:** Ceteris paribus, managers with high STEM skills perform better in strategic decision-making tasks when the task is presented in an abstract form compared to a contextualized form.

**H3:** Ceteris paribus, managerial performance improves more over repeated instances of a strategic decision-making task when the task is presented in an abstract rather than a contextualized form.

## 3. Experimental Design

### 3.1 Sample

- **Primary sample:** Prolific participants
- **Validation sample:** UZH Master students (with Advanced Microeconomics grades)
- **Compensation:** £9/hour (Prolific) + £2.25 base + potential $100 bonus

### 3.2 Task Design

Modified "Alien Game" (Billinger et al., 2014) - NK-based experimental platform:

- **Products:** 4 independent products
- **Attributes:** 3 binary attributes per product
- **Search space:** 2³ = 8 configurations per product
- **Rounds:** 8 periods per product lifecycle
- **Objective:** Maximize cumulative profit

### 3.3 Treatment Conditions

**Within-subjects design** (all participants complete all 4 tasks):

| | Section 1 | Section 2 |
|---|---|---|
| **Abstract** | Factors A, B, C in Country A | Factors D, E, F in Country B |
| **Context** | Lemonade Stand Austria (color, flavor, carbonation) | Lemonade Stand Germany (color, flavor, sweetener) |

### 3.4 Measures

- **Performance:** Cumulative profit across all rounds
- **Search behavior:** Hamming distance (how many attributes changed from best-known alternative)
- **Learning:** Performance improvement Section 1 → Section 2
- **STEM skills:** UZH Advanced Microeconomics exam grades

## 4. Results

### 4.1 Main Effects (H1)

**Finding:** Context framing improves performance

| Comparison | Section 1 | Section 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Abstract vs Context | Not significant | **p = 0.008** (Context > Abstract) |

### 4.2 Learning Effects

**Finding:** Both conditions improve over time, but abstract shows steeper learning

| Condition | S1 → S2 Improvement |
|---|---|
| Abstract | **p = 0.023** |
| Context | p = 0.01 |

### 4.3 STEM Moderation (H2)

**Finding:** High STEM performs better in abstract; effect reverses in context

- Participants with higher Advanced Microeconomics grades: Better cumulative profits in abstract
- Lower-scoring participants: Higher performance in contextualized condition (sometimes exceeded high-scorers)

### 4.4 Search Behavior Patterns

**Explore-Exploit Dynamics (Hamming Distance):**

| Condition | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Context | Early exploration → later exploitation (consistent across sections) |
| Abstract S1 | Weak explore-exploit pattern |
| Abstract S2 | **Strong explore-exploit pattern** (converges toward context pattern) |

**Key insight:** Abstract framing develops effective search strategies through learning, approximating the intuitive patterns that context framing enables from the start.

## 5. Discussion

### 5.1 Theoretical Contributions

1. **Micro-foundation of strategic decision making:** How information framing affects performance and learning
2. **Bridges Wason tradition with strategic management:** Context improves immediate reasoning
3. **Learning dynamics:** Abstraction supports cumulative capability building despite lower initial performance

### 5.2 Practical Implications

**For Executive Education:**

| Pedagogical Goal | Recommended Approach |
|---|---|
| Immediate comprehension | Context-rich cases |
| Pattern transfer | Structured abstraction exercises |
| Sustained capability | **Alternate between both** |

### 5.3 Individual Differences

STEM background moderates optimal framing:
- High STEM → Leverage abstract presentations
- Low STEM → Provide contextual anchors
