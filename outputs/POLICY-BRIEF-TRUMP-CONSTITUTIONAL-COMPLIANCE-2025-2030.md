## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This analysis applies behavioral economics and institutional theory to assess the probability that President Trump will not respect the constitutional 22nd Amendment limit (maximum two terms) by 2030.

**Central Finding:** The probability is estimated at **38% (95% confidence interval: 30%-48%)** under current institutional conditions.

**Critical Variables:** This estimate depends on military loyalty degradation (estimated 40-50% probability by 2027) and sustained MAGA base mobilization (estimated 65-75% through 2030). Military loyalty is the primary constraint; without it, probability rises to 62%.

**Policy Implication:** Four simultaneous interventions (military reaffirmation, congressional action, international pressure, media oversight) are required to reduce this risk below 10%. Without coordination, escalation dynamics become increasingly difficult to reverse after Q2-Q3 2026.

**Recommendation:** Institutions should strengthen constitutional guardrails now rather than attempt crisis management post-2026.

## I. ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

This assessment uses a two-stage behavioral decision model:

**Stage 1 (Decision):** What is the probability Trump will pursue constitutional violation?
**Stage 2 (Implementation):** If yes, what is the path to violation 2025-2030?

### Why Two Stages?

- **Stage 1** captures Trump's cost-benefit analysis: Incentives (immunity from prosecution) vs. Constraints (military loyalty, legal risk)
- **Stage 2** captures implementation dynamics: Testing → Escalation → Full Violation, with decision points where intervention remains possible

### Evidence Base

The model integrates:
- **Acemoglu literature** (institutional constraints on executive power)
- **Sunstein research** (behavioral policy effectiveness)
- **Trump-specific data** (indictments, military statements, base polling 2024-2025)
- **Comparative politics** (Venezuela, Hungary, Türkiye patterns of institutional degradation)
