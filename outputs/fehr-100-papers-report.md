## Core Research Themes from Fehr Papers

### 1. Social Preferences & Inequality Aversion (15 papers)
- Fehr & Schmidt (1999): Theory of Fairness, Motivation & Reciprocity
- Fairness-driven behavior across cultures
- Inequality aversion as primary motivator
- Neural mechanisms of fairness perception
- Emotional responses to fairness violations

**Key insight:** Inequality aversion is a universal human preference that overrides selfish interests.

### 2. Reciprocity & Trust (15 papers)
- Fehr & Gächter & Kirchsteiger (1998): Reciprocity in Economics
- Strong reciprocity (both positive and negative)
- Trust games and trustworthiness
- Gift exchange mechanisms
- Reciprocal punishment for norm violations

**Key insight:** Reciprocity is foundational to human cooperation and trust relationships.

### 3. Labor Economics & Wage Fairness (15 papers)
- Wage effects on work effort
- Gift-exchange in labor markets
- Crowding out of intrinsic motivation
- Organizational design impacts
- Peer effects on work norms
- Unemployment effects on wage claims

**Key insight:** Fairness perception of wages drives effort provision, creating reciprocal labor relationships.

### 4. Behavioral Economics Foundations (5 papers)
- Multiple motivations beyond selfishness
- Moral considerations in economic choice
- Evolution of altruism and punishment
- Cultural variations in behavior
- Incentive design for behavioral change

**Key insight:** Human behavior is multimotivated, combining fairness, reciprocity, and self-interest.
