## 1. Arguments to include in an application

When applying for an Innosuisse Innovation Cheque (CHF 15'000 support for collaboration with a research partner), the application should convincingly show:

### a) Innovation Potential

- What makes the project idea technologically or business-wise novel?
- How does it differ from existing solutions in the market (technology, process, or business model)?
- What is the innovative element that research (your research partner) will validate?

### b) Market Relevance

- What concrete problem does the project solve?
- Who are the target customers, users, or industry stakeholders that would benefit?
- What is the size and attractiveness of the potential market?
- What impact can be expected (efficiency gains, cost savings, sustainability improvements)?

### c) Feasibility & Risk Reduction

- What aspect of feasibility (technical, scientific, regulatory) needs to be tested?
- What is the proof-of-concept or prototype that can realistically be achieved with CHF 15'000?
- How exactly will the research partner's work reduce these risks?
- What would be the next steps if feasibility is proven?

### d) Collaboration with a Research Partner

- What scientific expertise is required that we don't have in-house?
- Why is the chosen research partner the most suitable for this project?
- What is the specific contribution of the research partner (methods, tools, facilities, know-how)?
- How will this collaboration strengthen the company's innovation capacity?
- Could this collaboration lead to a longer-term partnership or a follow-up Innosuisse project?

### e) Impact for Switzerland

- How does the project strengthen Swiss industry competitiveness (know-how, jobs, IP in Switzerland)?
- How does it support SME-university collaboration and knowledge transfer?
- What are the potential societal or economic benefits for Switzerland if successful?
- Could the project help position Switzerland as a leader in this innovation area?

## 2. Process to Apply for an Innovation Cheque

| Step | Bezeichnung | Beschreibung |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **1** | Eligibility Check | Applicant must be a Swiss SME or startup and the project must involve a research partner. The cheque is intended for preliminary studies, analyses, or prototype validations, not full-scale development. |
| **2** | Application for Mentor Support (optional) | The mentor supports your application for up to 35 hours of free services. You just need to officially request a mentor. See the information about the process and a list of accredited mentors. |
| **3** | Identify a Research Partner | Approach an eligible research partner. Your Mentor might help in finding the appropriate research partner and to define a small, concrete research activity that the cheque can finance. |
| **4** | Prepare Application | Applications are submitted through Innosuisse's online platform **Innolink**. This requires registration for an account to login and access the application form. |
| **5** | Submit to Innosuisse | Submission is continuous (no fixed deadlines). Processing time is 4-6 weeks. |
| **6** | Project Execution | If accepted, the research partner invoices Innosuisse directly (not the SME). Project time: 3-6 months. |
| **7** | Follow-up Opportunities | Results may lead to a full Innosuisse innovation project application. |

## 3. Innolink Application Form Structure

The form includes the following sections and questions:

### 1. Introduction
- Application name
- Abstract
- Context of application
- etc.

### 2. Organisations & People
- Implementation Partner
- Research Partner
- Project Manager
- Other Contacts & Collaborators
- Innovation Mentor

### 3. Value Creation & Solution
- Describe your innovative idea!
- How does it differ from existing solutions or products?
- What is the research partner's contribution?
- Describe the additional value provided by the research partner for your company!

---

## 4. Tips for the Application

- **Keep the project focused, realistic, and research-driven**
- **Define a measurable outcome**
- **Show that the Innovation Cheque is a low-cost, high-impact enabler** to explore feasibility and open doors for larger projects

## 5. Key Facts

| Aspekt | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Fördersumme** | CHF 15'000 |
| **Zielgruppe** | Schweizer KMU und Startups |
| **Zweck** | Vorstudien, Analysen, Prototyp-Validierungen |
| **NICHT für** | Vollständige Produktentwicklung |
| **Bearbeitungszeit** | 4-6 Wochen |
| **Projektdauer** | 3-6 Monate |
| **Einreichung** | Laufend (keine Fristen) |
| **Portal** | [Innolink](https://innolink.innosuisse.ch) |
| **Fakturierung** | Forschungspartner → Innosuisse (nicht an KMU) |
