## Missing Behavioral Mechanisms

This document fundamentally ignores core behavioral economics principles:

### 1. **Selection Bias & Confirmation Bias**
The N=9 from 188 papers represents classic **confirmation bias** - likely selecting only evidence supporting pre-existing beliefs. No mention of **availability heuristic** influencing which papers were "memorable" or accessible.

### 2. **Overconfidence Effect**
Authors exhibit **planning fallacy** by assuming APE insights transfer directly to consulting contexts without considering implementation barriers or **status quo bias** in organizational settings.

### 3. **Loss Aversion Ignored**
The "Ideen-Kill" system completely ignores **loss aversion** - decision makers will resist killing ideas they've invested effort in (sunk cost fallacy). No mechanism to overcome **endowment effect** for pet projects.

### 4. **Social Proof & Authority Bias**
Heavy reliance on Stanford APE research shows **authority bias**. The 3-reviewer system may create **herding behavior** rather than independent judgment due to **anchoring effects**.
