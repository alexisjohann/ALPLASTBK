Title: Behavioral Economics of Education Incentives

Journal: Journal of Human Capital
Year: 2011

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper applies behavioral economics to education incentives. We examine
how present bias, social influences, and cognitive limitations affect
educational choices. Understanding these factors is crucial for designing
effective education policies and incentive schemes.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. PRESENT BIAS IN EDUCATION
   - Underinvestment in education
   - Procrastination problems
   - Dropout risk

2. SOCIAL INFLUENCES
   - Peer effects significant
   - Parental expectations matter
   - Role model effects

3. INTERVENTION DESIGN
   - Commitment devices help
   - Financial incentives complex
   - Non-monetary incentives valuable
