Title: Contracting for Motivation and Trust
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Klaus M. Schmidt
Journal: Journal of Economic Psychology
Year: 2007

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how contract design affects motivation and trust.
We argue that contracts can signal trust or distrust, affecting intrinsic
motivation and cooperation. The choice of contract structure should account
for these behavioral effects beyond pure incentive alignment.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. CONTRACTS SIGNAL TRUST
   - Restrictive contracts signal distrust
   - Flexible contracts signal trust
   - Self-fulfilling prophecies

2. MOTIVATION EFFECTS
   - Trust enhances intrinsic motivation
   - Distrust crowds out motivation
   - Control paradox

3. OPTIMAL DESIGN
   - Balance control and trust
   - Context determines optimum
   - Consider signaling effects
