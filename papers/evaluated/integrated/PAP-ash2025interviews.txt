ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Interviews allow employers to learn about workers, but do they also enable
WORKERS TO LEARN ABOUT FIRMS?

DATA: 500,000 interview reports from Glassdoor

KEY FINDING - BIDIRECTIONAL INFORMATION:
Candidates for high-paying jobs are MORE LIKELY TO REJECT a job offer if
they believe the interview was EASY.

WHY EASY INTERVIEWS SIGNAL POOR FIT:
- Those who accept offers after easy interviews are:
  - 2/5 of a standard deviation LESS SATISFIED with their jobs
  - 10% LESS LIKELY to remain with employer for at least one year

LLM NARRATIVE ANALYSIS REVEALS:
- DIFFICULT interviews signal COLLEAGUE ABILITY
- EASY interviews convey a NONSELECTIVE PROCESS

FIELD EXPERIMENT:
Exogenous increase in difficulty:
- Elevated perceived difficulty
- BOOSTED applicant engagement with the vacancy

POLICY IMPLICATION:
Interviews offer workers a PREVIEW OF MATCH QUALITY.
Labor markets may become LESS EFFICIENT if firms automate hiring with AI
(making interviews too easy).

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Interviews are BIDIRECTIONAL information channels
2. Easy interviews → -0.4 SD job satisfaction
3. Easy interviews → -10% one-year retention
4. Difficult interviews signal colleague ability
5. RCT: Increasing difficulty boosts engagement
6. AI-automated hiring may reduce market efficiency
