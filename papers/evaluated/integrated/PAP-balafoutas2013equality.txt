Equality, Equity and Incentives: An Experiment
================================================================================

Authors: Balafoutas, Loukas and Kocher, Martin G and Putterman, Louis and Sutter, Matthias
Year: 2013
Journal: European Economic Review, 60(C), 32-51
DOI: 10.1016/j.euroecorev.2013.01.005

Source: Abstract fetched via WebSearch (2026-01-30)

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper studies a fundamental conflict in economic decision-making: the
trade-off between equality, equity and incentives, in a new experimental game
that nests a voluntary contributions mechanism in a broader spectrum of
incentive schemes.

In a 2x2 design, subjects either vote on or exogenously encounter incentive
settings while assigned unequal incomes that are either task-determined or
random.

The study examines tensions between egalitarianism, equity concerns, self-
interest, and the need for incentives.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

- Earned endowments lead to LESS support for redistribution
- Earned endowments lead to LESS cooperation
- Voting behavior is mainly self-interested but also influenced by:
  * Egalitarian concerns
  * Equity concerns (which sometimes cut in opposite directions)
- Cooperation rates respond continuously to incentive strength
- Contributions determined by:
  * Boundedly rational responses to incentives
  * Egalitarian considerations
  * Equity considerations
  * Strategic considerations

================================================================================
METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

- Study Design: Laboratory Experiment (2x2 factorial design)
- Sample: Experimental subjects
- Identification: Between-subject design
- Key manipulations:
  * Voting vs. exogenous incentive setting
  * Earned (task-determined) vs. random income assignment
- Game: Voluntary contributions mechanism nested in broader incentive spectrum
