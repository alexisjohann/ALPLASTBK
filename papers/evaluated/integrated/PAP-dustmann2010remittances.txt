Title: Remittances and Temporary Migration
Authors: Christian Dustmann, Josep Mestres
Year: 2010
Journal: Journal of Development Economics, Vol. 92, No. 1, pp. 62-70
DOI: 10.1016/j.jdeveco.2008.12.002

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper studies the remittance behavior of immigrants and how it relates to
temporary versus permanent migration plans.

The authors use a unique data source that provides unusual detail on remittances
and return plans, and follows the same household over time. The data allows
them to distinguish between different PURPOSES of remittances.

They analyze the association between:
- Individual and household characteristics
- Geographic location of the family
- Return plans
- Remittances

The panel nature of the data allows conditioning on household fixed effects.
To address measurement error and reverse causality, they use an instrumental
variable estimator.

MAIN RESULT: Changes in return plans are related to LARGE CHANGES in
remittance flows.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Return migration plans strongly predict remittance behavior
2. Changes in plans → large changes in remittance flows
3. Panel data with household fixed effects for identification
4. IV estimator addresses measurement error and reverse causality
5. Distinguishes different purposes of remittances
6. Unique data: follows same households over time
