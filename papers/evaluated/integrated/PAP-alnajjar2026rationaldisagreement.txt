Title: Rational Disagreement
Authors: Nabil Al-Najjar, Harald Uhlig
Year: 2026
Journal: NBER Working Paper No. 34727
DOI: 10.3386/w34727

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The authors propose rational disagreement as a formal framework for analyzing
seemingly irrational behavior that can persist despite the wide availability
of objective information in a steady-state.

Agents are RATIONAL in that they correctly anticipate the distribution of
aggregate outcomes, yet DISAGREE about which specific individuals perform
better than others.

Notably, the subjective belief of any individual may be objectively correct.

The paper illustrates the key concepts with a simple entry game, shows how
unordered individual outcome distributions can be identified solely from
aggregate statistics, then characterizes the resulting game, defines its
Nash equilibria, and develops a statistical test for the null hypothesis
of agreement.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Rational disagreement can persist in steady-state with objective information
2. Agents correctly anticipate aggregate outcomes but disagree on individuals
3. Subjective beliefs may be objectively correct
4. Framework applies to entry games and market behavior
5. Nash equilibria characterization under disagreement
6. Statistical test developed for null hypothesis of agreement

METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTION

- Formal framework for analyzing persistent disagreement

- Application to microeconomic behavior patterns
