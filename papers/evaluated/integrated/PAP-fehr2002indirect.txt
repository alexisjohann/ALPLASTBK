Title: Strong Reciprocity, Human Cooperation, and the Enforcement of Social Norms
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Urs Fischbacher, Simon Gächter
Journal: Human Nature
Year: 2002

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper presents a comprehensive theory of strong reciprocity and its role
in human cooperation. Strong reciprocators cooperate in one-shot interactions
and punish norm violators at personal cost. We review experimental evidence
showing that strong reciprocity is key to understanding the enforcement of
social norms and the evolution of human cooperation.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. STRONG RECIPROCITY DEFINED
   - Cooperation in one-shot games
   - Punishment of defectors
   - Costly to the punisher

2. NORM ENFORCEMENT
   - Decentralized punishment works
   - No central authority needed
   - Self-sustaining equilibria

3. EXPERIMENTAL EVIDENCE
   - Public goods games
   - Third-party punishment
   - Cross-cultural validity

4. EVOLUTIONARY BASIS
   - Not explained by self-interest
   - Cultural group selection
   - Gene-culture coevolution
