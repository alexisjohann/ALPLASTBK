ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This chapter presents a TRACTABLE FRAMEWORK for the study of technology
adoption and diffusion in the context of economic development.

CORE MECHANISM:
Firms in countries behind the WORLD TECHNOLOGY FRONTIER can rapidly adopt
new techniques from the frontier.

FOUR BARRIERS SLOW DOWN TECHNOLOGY ADOPTION:

1. LOW ABSORPTIVE CAPACITY
   - Weak education systems
   - Poor management practices
   - Barriers to technology adoption

2. INSTITUTIONAL DISTORTIONS
   - Policy and regulatory barriers

3. INAPPROPRIATE TECHNOLOGY
   - Mismatch between frontier technologies and local needs

4. CREDIT MARKET FRICTIONS
   - Financial constraints on adoption

CONSEQUENCES:
- Greater DISTANCE TO FRONTIER
- Lower INCOME PER CAPITA
- BUT: Long-run GROWTH RATE still equals frontier (catch-up possible)

EXTENSIONS:
- Choice between INNOVATION and IMITATION
- Role of SELECTION for higher-productivity firms
- Higher-absorptive capacity firms during development

EMPIRICAL ILLUSTRATION:
Cross-country and firm-level data correlations support framework.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Four barriers: absorptive capacity, institutions, inappropriate tech, credit
2. Barriers increase distance to frontier and lower GDP/capita
3. Long-run growth rate STILL EQUALS frontier (catch-up possible)
4. Innovation vs imitation choice matters
5. Selection favors high-productivity, high-absorptive capacity firms
6. Tractable framework with empirical support
