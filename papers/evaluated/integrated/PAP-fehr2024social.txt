ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This comprehensive JEL review synthesizes decades of research on social
preferences. We examine the fundamental characteristics of fairness, reciprocity,
and other-regarding preferences, and their economic consequences. The paper
provides an authoritative overview of the state of the field.
