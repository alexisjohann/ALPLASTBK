================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines the relationship between income inequality and social
norms. We argue that high inequality can undermine social norms of cooperation
and fairness, while strong cooperative norms can help reduce inequality.
This creates potential for both vicious and virtuous cycles.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. INEQUALITY → NORMS
   - High inequality weakens cooperation norms
   - Perceived unfairness erodes trust
   - Social cohesion declines

2. NORMS → INEQUALITY
   - Strong norms enable redistribution
   - Cooperation reduces inequality
   - Social investment increases

3. FEEDBACK DYNAMICS
   - Vicious cycles possible
   - Virtuous cycles possible
   - Tipping points matter

4. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Address inequality early
   - Invest in social norms
   - Design for virtuous cycles
