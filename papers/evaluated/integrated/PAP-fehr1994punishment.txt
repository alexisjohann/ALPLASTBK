Title: Costly Punishment Sustains Cooperation
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization
Year: 1994

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper demonstrates that costly punishment can sustain cooperation in
social dilemmas. In public goods experiments, we show that when participants
can punish free-riders at personal cost, cooperation levels remain high.
Without punishment opportunities, cooperation declines over time.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. PUNISHMENT EFFECTIVENESS
   - Costly punishment sustains cooperation
   - Cooperation stable with punishment
   - Declines without punishment option

2. WHO PUNISHES
   - Strong reciprocators punish
   - Punishment proportional to violation
   - Emotional component involved

3. IMPLICATIONS
   - Decentralized enforcement possible
   - No central authority needed
   - Self-organizing cooperation
