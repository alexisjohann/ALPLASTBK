Title: Meta-Analysis: The Impact of Immigration on the Economic Performance of the Host Country
Authors: Alexandre Luz, Pedro Cunha Neves, Oscar Afonso, Elena Sochirca
Year: 2025
Journal: Economies (MDPI), Vol. 13, No. 8, pp. 1-21
DOI: 10.3390/economies13080213

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Growing global migration flows highlight the importance of understanding their
economic impact. While many studies have explored how immigration affects host
countries' macroeconomic indicators, the results are mixed.

The authors develop a META-ANALYSIS to investigate the effect of immigration
on the economic performance of the host country, focusing on key indicators:
- Economic growth
- Productivity
- Unemployment
- Innovation

KEY FINDING: On average, immigration has a POSITIVE and STATISTICALLY
SIGNIFICANT impact on economic performance.

The effect varies based on:
- Immigrant characteristics (qualifications, age)
- Host country characteristics (economic development level)
- Methodological approaches (contributing to heterogeneity)

SCOPE: This is the FIRST study to examine immigration's effects on a broad
range of economic performance indicators (beyond just wages/labor market).

DATABASE: 41 primary studies, 1,459 individual estimates.
No clear evidence of publication bias found.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Immigration has POSITIVE average effect on economic performance
2. First meta-analysis beyond labor market effects
3. 41 studies, 1,459 estimates analyzed
4. No publication bias detected
5. Heterogeneity explained by immigrant and host characteristics
6. Methodological differences contribute to mixed findings
