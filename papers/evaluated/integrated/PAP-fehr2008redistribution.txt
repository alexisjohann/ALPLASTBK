Title: Redistribution, Voting, and Inequality
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Review of Economic Studies
Year: 2008

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how inequality affects voting for redistribution.
We show that inequality aversion and fairness concerns shape political
preferences beyond narrow self-interest. This helps explain voting patterns
that standard models cannot account for.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. VOTING AND FAIRNESS
   - Not purely self-interested
   - Inequality aversion matters
   - Perceived fairness of inequality

2. INEQUALITY EFFECTS
   - High inequality → more support
   - Perception of causes matters
   - Mobility beliefs

3. POLITICAL IMPLICATIONS
   - Explains "puzzle" voting
   - Social preferences in politics
   - Policy feedback
