================================================================================
Choosing the Carrot or the Stick? Endogenous Institutional Choice in Social Dilemma Situations
================================================================================

Authors: Sutter, Matthias and Haigner, Stefan and Kocher, Martin G
Year: 2010
Journal: The Review of Economic Studies, 77(4), 1540-1566
DOI: 10.1111/j.1467-937X.2010.00608.x

Source: Abstract fetched via WebSearch (2026-01-30)

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The paper analyzes an experimental public goods game in which group members can
endogenously determine whether they want to supplement a standard voluntary
contribution mechanism with the possibility of rewarding or punishing other
group members.

The authors find a significantly positive effect of endogenous institutional
choice on the level of cooperation in comparison to the same exogenously
implemented institutions. This suggests that participation rights enhance
cooperation in groups.

With endogenous choice, groups typically vote for the reward option.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

- Endogenous institutional choice significantly increases cooperation compared
  to exogenously imposed institutions
- Participation rights (voting on rules) enhance group cooperation
- When given the choice, groups typically prefer rewards (carrots) over
  punishments (sticks)
- The process of choosing institutions matters, not just the institution itself

================================================================================
METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

- Study Design: Laboratory Experiment (Public Goods Game)
- Sample: Experimental subjects in controlled lab setting
- Identification: Between-subject comparison of endogenous vs. exogenous
  institutional choice
- Key manipulation: Groups vote on whether to add reward/punishment mechanisms
