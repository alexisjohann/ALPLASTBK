Title: Detrimental Effects of Sanctions on Human Altruism
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Bettina Rockenbach
Journal: Nature
Year: 2003

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper shows that incentive schemes can backfire by undermining intrinsic
motivation. When principals have the option to sanction agents but choose not
to use it, cooperation increases. However, when sanctions are actually imposed,
they crowd out intrinsic motivation and reduce cooperation. This demonstrates
the importance of trust signals in economic relationships.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. CROWDING OUT EFFECT
   - Sanctions reduce intrinsic motivation
   - Trust signals increase cooperation
   - Incentives can backfire

2. TRUST AND COOPERATION
   - Not using punishment signals trust
   - Trust increases effort
   - Self-fulfilling prophecy

3. MECHANISM
   - Sanctions signal distrust
   - Workers reduce effort in response
   - Intrinsic motivation crowded out

4. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Careful with incentive design
   - Trust may be more effective
   - Sanctions as last resort
