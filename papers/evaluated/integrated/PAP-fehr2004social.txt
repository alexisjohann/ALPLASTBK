ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Social norms are behavioral rules that are partly enforced through sanctions
from other members of the community. We argue that understanding the nature
of social norm enforcement is crucial for understanding human cooperation.
Recent evidence from experimental economics and neuroscience suggests that
humans have a strong predisposition to enforce social norms even at personal
cost.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. SOCIAL NORMS DEFINED
   - Behavioral standards shared by a community
   - Enforced through social sanctions (approval/disapproval)
   - Internalized as preferences/emotions

2. NORM ENFORCEMENT MECHANISMS
   - Direct punishment of violators
   - Third-party punishment
   - Exclusion from future interactions
   - Reputation damage

3. COGNITIVE BASIS
   - Emotion systems (anger at violations)
   - Theory of mind (intent attribution)
   - Cultural learning (norm acquisition)

4. NEURAL CORRELATES
   - Prefrontal cortex in norm compliance
   - Striatum in reward from punishment
   - Insula in disgust at unfairness
