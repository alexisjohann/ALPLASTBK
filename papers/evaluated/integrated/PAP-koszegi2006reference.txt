Title: A Model of Reference-Dependent Preferences
Authors: Botond Kőszegi, Matthew Rabin
Year: 2006
Journal: Quarterly Journal of Economics, Vol. 121, No. 4, pp. 1133-1165

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The authors develop a model of reference-dependent preferences and loss aversion
where "gain-loss utility" is derived from standard "consumption utility" and the
reference point is determined endogenously by the economic environment.

Most importantly, they posit that a person's reference point is her recent
EXPECTATIONS about outcomes (rather than the status quo), and assume behavior
accords to a personal equilibrium: the person maximizes utility given her
rational expectations about outcomes, where these expectations depend on her
own anticipated behavior.

In deterministic environments, choices maximize consumption utility, but
gain-loss utility influences behavior when there is uncertainty. The reference
point is determined by rational expectations held in the recent past about
outcomes, which must be consistent with optimal behavior given expectations.

Applications to consumer behavior show willingness to pay increases with
expected probability of purchase and expected prices. In labor supply, workers
are less likely to continue if income is unexpectedly high (reference point
anchoring).

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. EXPECTATIONS AS REFERENCE: Reference point is rational expectations about
   outcomes, not status quo

2. PERSONAL EQUILIBRIUM: Behavior consistent with rational expectations about
   own behavior

3. ENDOGENOUS REFERENCE: Reference point determined by economic environment,
   not exogenous

4. ATTACHMENT EFFECT: WTP increases with expected probability of purchase

5. LABOR SUPPLY: Unexpected high income reduces work continuation

6. UNCERTAINTY MATTERS: Gain-loss utility active only under uncertainty
