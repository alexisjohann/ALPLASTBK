ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This handbook chapter summarizes major findings from behavioral and
experimental economics. We review key results on social preferences,
cooperation, and decision-making that have transformed economic thinking.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. SOCIAL PREFERENCES
   - Inequality aversion established
   - Reciprocity documented
   - Type heterogeneity

2. COOPERATION
   - Public goods experiments
   - Punishment sustains cooperation
   - Institutions matter

3. DECISION MAKING
   - Bounded rationality confirmed
   - Framing effects robust
   - Context sensitivity
