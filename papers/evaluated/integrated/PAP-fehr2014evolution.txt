Authors: Ernst Fehr

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This Nature Human Behaviour article examines the evolutionary origins of
altruism and punishment. We analyze how these traits could evolve and
their role in sustaining human cooperation at scale.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. EVOLUTIONARY MECHANISMS
   - Group selection possible
   - Cultural evolution important
   - Gene-culture coevolution

2. PUNISHMENT EVOLUTION
   - Altruistic punishment costly
   - Reputation effects help
   - Institutional enforcement

3. SCALE OF COOPERATION
   - Human uniqueness
   - Large-group cooperation
   - Cultural transmission
