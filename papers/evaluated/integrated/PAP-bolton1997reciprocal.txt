Title: ERC: A Theory of Equity, Reciprocity, and Competition
Authors: Gary E. Bolton, Axel Ockenfels
Year: 2000
Journal: American Economic Review, Vol. 90, No. 1, pp. 166-193
DOI: 10.1257/aer.90.1.166

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The authors demonstrate that a simple model, constructed on the premise that
people are motivated by both their pecuniary payoff and their relative payoff
standing, organizes a large and seemingly disparate set of laboratory
observations as one consistent pattern.

The model is incomplete information but nevertheless posed entirely in terms of
directly observable variables. The model explains observations from games where
equity is thought to be a factor, such as ultimatum and dictator, games where
reciprocity is thought to play a role, such as the prisoner's dilemma and gift
exchange, and games where competitive behavior is observed, such as Bertrand
markets.

The ERC model posits that, along with pecuniary gain, people are motivated by
their own relative payoff standing. Beyond this, ERC employs standard game
theoretic concepts.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. DUAL MOTIVATION: People care about both absolute payoff and relative
   standing

2. ERC MODEL: Equity, Reciprocity, Competition framework unifies diverse
   experimental findings

3. ULTIMATUM/DICTATOR: Model explains rejection of unfair offers and
   voluntary giving

4. PRISONER'S DILEMMA: Explains cooperation when relative payoffs matter

5. GIFT EXCHANGE: Explains why workers reciprocate high wages with high effort

6. COMPETITIVE MARKETS: Model consistent with competitive outcomes in market
   settings (Bertrand)
