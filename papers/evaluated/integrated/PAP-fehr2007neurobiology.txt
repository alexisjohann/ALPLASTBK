ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper reviews the emerging field of social neuroeconomics - the study
of how neural processes underlie social and economic decision-making.
We examine brain regions involved in trust, fairness, cooperation, and
punishment. Understanding the neural basis of social behavior provides
new foundations for economic theory.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. NEURAL BASIS OF TRUST
   - Prefrontal cortex involvement
   - Oxytocin effects
   - Betrayal responses

2. FAIRNESS AND THE BRAIN
   - Anterior insula activated by unfairness
   - Emotional responses automatic
   - Cognitive control modulates responses

3. COOPERATION CIRCUITS
   - Reward systems activated
   - Social utility represented
   - Strategic thinking regions

4. PUNISHMENT PROCESSING
   - Dorsal striatum activation
   - Satisfying punishment
   - Costly punishment rewarding
