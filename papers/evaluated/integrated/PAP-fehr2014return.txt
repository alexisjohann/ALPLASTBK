ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The psychological consequences of poverty may impede escape from poverty.
This paper reviews evidence that poverty affects cognitive function, time
preferences, and risk attitudes. Scarcity consumes mental bandwidth, leading
to poor decision-making. Understanding these psychological effects is crucial
for designing effective poverty alleviation policies.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. COGNITIVE EFFECTS OF POVERTY
   - Scarcity reduces cognitive bandwidth
   - Poor performance on fluid intelligence tests
   - Reduced executive control
   - Effects are causal, not just correlational

2. TIME PREFERENCES
   - Poverty increases present bias
   - Shorter planning horizons
   - More hyperbolic discounting

3. RISK ATTITUDES
   - Poverty may increase risk aversion
   - Or increase risk-seeking (desperation)
   - Depends on context and framing

4. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Simplify decisions for the poor
   - Reduce cognitive load in programs
   - Account for psychological constraints
