Title: Existence of an Equilibrium for a Competitive Economy
Authors: Kenneth J. Arrow, Gerard Debreu
Year: 1954
Journal: Econometrica, Vol. 22, No. 3, pp. 265-290
DOI: 10.2307/1907353

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

FOUNDATIONAL PAPER establishing the existence of general equilibrium.

Arrow and Debreu prove that under certain conditions, a competitive economy
has at least one equilibrium state. The proof uses Kakutani's fixed point
theorem. This paper formalized the conditions under which Adam Smith's
"invisible hand" - the coordination of individual self-interest to produce
socially beneficial outcomes - actually works.

The conditions include: convex preferences, convex production sets, and
appropriate continuity assumptions. The paper established the mathematical
foundations for much of modern economic theory.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. EXISTENCE PROOF: First rigorous proof that competitive equilibrium exists
   under well-specified conditions

2. FIXED POINT APPROACH: Uses Kakutani's fixed point theorem to prove existence

3. CONVEXITY REQUIREMENTS: Convex preferences and production sets are
   necessary conditions

4. WALRASIAN EQUILIBRIUM: Formalizes conditions for price-mediated coordination

5. FOUNDATIONAL: Established mathematical rigor for microeconomic theory

6. WELFARE THEOREMS: Enabled subsequent First and Second Welfare Theorem proofs
