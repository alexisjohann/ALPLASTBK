Title: The Red and the Black: Mental Accounting of Savings and Debt
Authors: Drazen Prelec, George Loewenstein
Year: 1998
Journal: Marketing Science, Vol. 17, No. 1, pp. 4-28

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper proposes a "double-entry" mental accounting theory that describes
the nature of reciprocal interactions between the pleasure of consumption and
the pain of paying, and draws out their implications for consumer behavior
and hedonics.

When people make purchases, they often experience an immediate pain of paying
that can undermine the pleasure derived from consumption. The ticking taxi
meter, for example, reduces one's pleasure from the ride.

Contrary to standard economic predictions that people finance purchases to
minimize present value of payments, the model predicts strong DEBT AVERSION:
people prefer to prepay for consumption or get paid for work after it is
performed. Such pay-before sequences allow consumption to be enjoyed "as if
it were free" and the pain of paying is mitigated by thoughts of future benefits.

The model also predicts preference for flat-rate pricing (unlimited Internet
at fixed monthly price) even if it involves paying more for the same usage -
this decouples consumption from payment pain.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. DOUBLE-ENTRY ACCOUNTING: Pleasure of consumption and pain of paying interact

2. DEBT AVERSION: Strong preference for prepayment over borrowing

3. PAYMENT PAIN: Immediate pain of paying undermines consumption pleasure

4. COUPLING: Close temporal link between payment and consumption increases pain

5. FLAT-RATE PREFERENCE: Prefer fixed fees even if more expensive (decoupling)

6. HEDONIC VS DECISION EFFICIENCY: Tension between emotional and rational
   optimization
