================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper reviews the economic and biological foundations of trust. Drawing
on neuroeconomics, behavioral economics, and evolutionary biology, we examine
how trust emerges, what its neural correlates are, and why it varies across
individuals and societies. Understanding the biology of trust provides new
insights for economic theory and policy.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. NEURAL BASIS OF TRUST
   - Trust activates reward systems
   - Oxytocin enhances trust
   - Betrayal triggers strong negative responses

2. INDIVIDUAL DIFFERENCES
   - Genetic variation matters
   - Early experiences shape trust
   - Cultural transmission

3. ECONOMIC IMPLICATIONS
   - Trust reduces transaction costs
   - Enables incomplete contracts
   - Foundational for cooperation

4. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Institutions can build trust
   - Cultural factors matter
   - Design for trustworthiness
