================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

FIRST LARGE-SCALE INCENTIVIZED PANEL of US firm forecasts.

DATA: Partnership with large US payment-processing company
- 5-year, 10-wave panel survey
- Incentivized quarterly sales forecasts
- Over 6,000 firms
- Matched to proprietary revenue data

FOUR FORECASTING BIASES DOCUMENTED:

1. INACCURACY: Win rate only 18%
2. OVER-OPTIMISM: +16.3% systematic positive bias
3. PREDICTABLE ERRORS: Errors are forecastable
4. OVER-PRECISION: Subjective variance = 50% of true variance

FOUR RANDOMIZED EXPERIMENTS:

1. DATA USE (Dashboard) → WORKS
   - Decreases noise by 15%
   - Reduces over-precision
   - +2 percentage points accuracy

2. INCENTIVES → WORKS
   - Higher incentives moderate over-optimism
   - $400 incentive → -70% optimism
   - +0.9pp accuracy per $100

3. FORECASTING SKILL TRAINING → NO EFFECT

4. CONTINGENT THINKING → NO EFFECT

KEY INSIGHT:
Biases are MOTIVATED (Bénabou-Tirole), not cognitive.
Can be reduced with incentives but NOT eliminated with training.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Win rate only 18% (firms forecast poorly)
2. Over-optimism +16.3%, over-precision 50% variance
3. Data dashboards work (+2pp accuracy)
4. Incentives work (-70% optimism at $400)
5. Training does NOT work
6. Biases are motivated, not cognitive
