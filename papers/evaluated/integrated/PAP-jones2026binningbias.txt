================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

CRITICAL METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTION to climate economics.

THE COMMON APPROACH:
Estimates of climate impacts count days in different temperature ranges
("bins") and consider how exposure affects outcomes. This often produces
NON-LINEAR, U-SHAPED RESULTS where high and low temperatures have
largest effects.

THE PROBLEM:
Non-linear approaches like these can generate SPURIOUS FINDINGS.

MECHANISM:
- Global warming induces TRENDS in extreme temperature exposure
- These trends correlate MECHANICALLY with a location's baseline temperature
- SUBSTANTIAL BIAS emerges if outcome trends also correlate with
  baseline temperature (for any reason)

DEMONSTRATION:
- Theoretically
- In simulations
- With real outcomes

SOLUTIONS DEVELOPED:
Corrections that account for this bias.

APPLICATION TO US DATA:
- Some results in literature are UNAFFECTED by corrections
- Other results CHANGE SUBSTANTIALLY

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Temperature binning can generate SPURIOUS U-shaped findings
2. Mechanical correlation between warming trends and baseline temp
3. Bias when outcome trends correlate with baseline temp
4. Demonstrated theoretically, in simulations, with real data
5. Solutions developed and applied
6. Some prior results change substantially with corrections
