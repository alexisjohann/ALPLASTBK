Title: Voluntary Provision of a Public Good
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Experimental Economics
Year: 1996

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines voluntary contributions to public goods in experimental
settings. We show that initial contributions are substantial but decline
over time unless communication or punishment opportunities are available.
Understanding these dynamics is key for public goods provision mechanisms.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. INITIAL CONTRIBUTIONS
   - Substantial voluntary provision
   - Above Nash equilibrium
   - Conditional cooperation

2. CONTRIBUTION DECAY
   - Decline over time
   - Free-rider problem emerges
   - End-game effects

3. SUSTAINING CONTRIBUTIONS
   - Communication helps
   - Punishment opportunities
   - Institutional design matters
