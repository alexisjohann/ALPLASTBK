Title: Return Migration, Uncertainty and Precautionary Savings
Authors: Christian Dustmann
Year: 1997
Journal: Journal of Development Economics, Vol. 52, No. 2, pp. 295-316
DOI: 10.1016/S0304-3878(96)00450-6

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper presents a life-cycle model where migrants determine RE-MIGRATION
and CONSUMPTION simultaneously in a STOCHASTIC ENVIRONMENT.

Whether precautionary savings of migrants are above or below those of natives
is AMBIGUOUS in general -- the sign depends on:
1. The risk in host- and home-country labor markets
2. The correlation of labor market shocks between countries

Furthermore, the effect of an uncertain environment on migration and
re-migration plans cannot be unambiguously signed for the general case.
It depends on:
- The size of the wage differential
- The relative risk the migrant exhibits in the two labor markets

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Simultaneous determination of return migration and consumption
2. Precautionary savings: migrants vs natives - ambiguous sign
3. Labor market risk in BOTH countries matters
4. CORRELATION of shocks affects decisions
5. Wage differential size influences uncertainty effects
6. Three key factors for return migration:
   a) Relative prices (home vs host)
   b) Subjective preferences
   c) Accumulated resources

================================================================================
THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTION
================================================================================

- Life-cycle model with stochastic environment
- Endogenizes return migration decision
- Explains why migrants may return despite higher wages abroad
- Precautionary savings framework applied to migration
- Based on European guest worker experience (Switzerland, Germany, France)
