Title: Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk
Authors: Daniel Kahneman, Amos Tversky
Year: 1979
Journal: Econometrica, Vol. 47, No. 2, pp. 263-291
DOI: 10.2307/1914185

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper presents a critique of expected utility theory as a descriptive
model of decision making under risk, and develops an alternative model,
called PROSPECT THEORY.

Choices among risky prospects exhibit several pervasive effects that are
inconsistent with the basic tenets of utility theory:

1. CERTAINTY EFFECT: People underweight outcomes that are merely probable
   in comparison with outcomes obtained with certainty. This contributes to:
   - Risk AVERSION in choices involving sure gains
   - Risk SEEKING in choices involving sure losses

2. VALUE FUNCTION with three key properties:
   - Defined on DEVIATIONS from a reference point (not final wealth)
   - Generally CONCAVE for gains and CONVEX for losses
   - STEEPER for losses than for gains (Loss Aversion)

3. PROBABILITY WEIGHTING: Non-linear transformation of probabilities
   - Overweighting of small probabilities
   - Underweighting of moderate/high probabilities

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Reference dependence: Value = f(change), not f(wealth)
2. Loss aversion parameter: λ ≈ 2.25
3. Diminishing sensitivity: α = β ≈ 0.88
4. Certainty effect drives risk preferences
5. Reflection effect: Risk attitudes reverse for gains vs losses
6. Most replicated finding in behavioral economics (1000+ replications)
