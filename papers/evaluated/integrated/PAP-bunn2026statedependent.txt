Title: State and Time-Dependent Pricing

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

New evidence on how firms set prices using direct questions from a large,
ECONOMY-WIDE SURVEY of UK firms.

KEY FINDING - SHIFT TO STATE-DEPENDENT PRICING:
- 2019: 44% of firms used state-dependent pricing
- 2023: 54% of firms use state-dependent pricing

WHO USES STATE-DEPENDENT PRICING?
- SMALLER firms
- Firms with HIGHER SHARE of non-labour costs
- Firms reporting HIGHER SUBJECTIVE UNCERTAINTY around sales and prices

IMPLICATIONS FOR INFLATION DYNAMICS:

State-dependent firms:
- Experienced SHARPER INCREASE in price growth (2022-2023)
- Also FASTER SUBSEQUENT DECLINE

EVIDENCE FROM:
- Randomized survey experiment
- Firm-level forecast errors
- Local projections

RESULT:
Prices of state-dependent firms RESPOND FASTER to cost shocks.

The difference between state-dependent and time-dependent firms is
LARGER FOR BIGGER SHOCKS (consistent with theory).

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. State-dependent pricing: 44% (2019) → 54% (2023)
2. Smaller firms, higher uncertainty → more state-dependent
3. State-dependent firms respond FASTER to cost shocks
4. Effect larger for bigger shocks (menu cost theory)
5. Sharper price increases AND faster declines
6. Survey + experiment + local projections evidence
