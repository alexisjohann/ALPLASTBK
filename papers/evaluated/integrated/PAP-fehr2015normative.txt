ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Human cooperation is unique in its scale and complexity. We argue that
social norms, internalized through socialization, provide the normative
foundations for human cooperation. These norms are enforced through
decentralized punishment and reward, creating stable cooperative equilibria.
Cultural evolution shapes which norms emerge and persist.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. NORMATIVE FOUNDATIONS
   - Norms as shared behavioral expectations
   - Internalization through socialization
   - Enforcement through social sanctions

2. MECHANISMS OF ENFORCEMENT
   - Punishment of violators
   - Reward of compliers
   - Reputation effects
   - Emotional responses (guilt, shame, anger)

3. CULTURAL EVOLUTION
   - Norms evolve through cultural transmission
   - Group selection of norm systems
   - Gene-culture coevolution

4. IMPLICATIONS
   - Institutions embed norms
   - Policy must work with existing norms
   - Norm change is possible but slow
