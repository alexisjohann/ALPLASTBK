Title: Inequality and Cooperation: Evidence from Labor Markets
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Journal of Labor Economics
Year: 2001

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how inequality affects cooperation in labor market
experiments. We show that wage inequality can both help and hurt cooperation,
depending on how it is perceived. When inequality is seen as fair (based on
productivity), it does not harm cooperation. When seen as unfair, it
undermines cooperation and efficiency.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. INEQUALITY AND FAIRNESS
   - Inequality per se not harmful
   - Perceived unfairness matters
   - Legitimacy of inequality key

2. LABOR MARKET EXPERIMENTS
   - Wage inequality varied
   - Effort responses measured
   - Gift exchange affected

3. IMPLICATIONS
   - Fair procedures important
   - Transparency helps
   - Equality not always required
