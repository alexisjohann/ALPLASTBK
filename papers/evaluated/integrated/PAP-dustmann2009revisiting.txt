Title: Revisiting the German Wage Structure
Authors: Christian Dustmann, Johannes Ludsteck, Uta Schönberg
Year: 2009
Journal: The Quarterly Journal of Economics, Vol. 124, No. 2, pp. 843-881
DOI: 10.1162/qjec.2009.124.2.843

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper shows that wage inequality in West Germany has increased over the
past three decades, contrary to common perceptions.

During the 1980s, the increase was concentrated at the TOP of the distribution.
In the 1990s, it occurred at the BOTTOM end as well.

The findings are consistent with the view that both in Germany and in the
United States, TECHNOLOGICAL CHANGE is responsible for the widening of the
wage distribution at the top.

At the bottom of the wage distribution, the increase in inequality is better
explained by EPISODIC EVENTS, such as supply shocks and changes in labor
market institutions.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. German wage inequality increased over 3 decades (contrary to perceptions)
2. 1980s: Inequality grew at TOP of distribution
3. 1990s: Inequality grew at BOTTOM of distribution as well
4. TOP inequality: Technological change (skill-biased) explanation
5. BOTTOM inequality: Episodic events (supply shocks, institutional changes)
6. US-Germany comparison: Similar patterns at top, reversed at bottom
7. Education/age structure changes explain substantial part of top inequality

================================================================================
CONTRIBUTION
================================================================================

- Challenges view of stable German wage structure
- Separates mechanisms at top vs bottom of distribution
- Shows timing matters: 80s vs 90s patterns differ
- Comparative perspective: US parallels and differences
