Title: Return Migration: Theory and Empirical Evidence from the UK
Authors: Christian Dustmann, Yoram Weiss
Year: 2007
Journal: British Journal of Industrial Relations, Vol. 45, No. 2, pp. 236-256
DOI: 10.1111/j.1467-8543.2007.00613.x

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

In this article, the authors discuss forms of migration that are non-permanent.
They focus on temporary migrations where the decision to return is taken by
the immigrant.

These migrations are likely to be frequent, and they provide some evidence
for the UK.

They develop a SIMPLE MODEL that rationalizes the decision of a migrant to
RETURN to his/her home country, DESPITE a persistently higher wage in the
host country.

THREE MOTIVES for temporary migration:
(i) Differences in RELATIVE PRICES between host and home country
(ii) COMPLEMENTARITIES between consumption and the location where consumption
    takes place
(iii) The possibility of accumulating HUMAN CAPITAL abroad, which enhances
     the immigrant's earnings potential back home

For the last return motive, they discuss extensions that allow for immigrant
heterogeneity, and develop implications for selective in- and out-migration.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Return migration is RATIONAL despite higher wages in host country
2. Three return motives: prices, consumption complementarities, human capital
3. Human capital motive predicts selective out-migration
4. UK data supports theoretical predictions
5. Framework explains temporary migration as optimal choice
6. Heterogeneity matters for selective migration patterns

================================================================================
THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTION
================================================================================

- Unified theoretical framework for return migration
- Three distinct mechanisms formalized
- Selective migration predictions derived
- Explains "paradox" of returning to lower wages
- Dynamic optimization approach
