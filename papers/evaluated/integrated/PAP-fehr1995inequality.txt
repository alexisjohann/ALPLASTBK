Title: Inequality Aversion Explains Much of Human Altruism
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Klaus M. Schmidt
Journal: American Economic Review
Year: 1995

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper argues that inequality aversion can explain much of human altruistic
behavior observed in experiments. Building on the Fehr-Schmidt model, we show
that aversion to disadvantageous and advantageous inequality provides a unified
explanation for behavior in ultimatum, dictator, and public goods games.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. INEQUALITY AVERSION
   - Aversion to being behind (α)
   - Aversion to being ahead (β)
   - α > β typically

2. EXPLANATORY POWER
   - Ultimatum game rejections
   - Dictator game giving
   - Public goods contributions

3. PREDICTIONS
   - Context matters
   - Distribution of types
   - Strategic interactions
