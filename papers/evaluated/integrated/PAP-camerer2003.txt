================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This chapter provides an introduction to the approach and methods of behavioral
economics, and to some of its major findings, applications, and promising new
directions.

Behavioral economics increases the explanatory power of economics by providing
it with more realistic psychological foundations. At the core of behavioral
economics is the conviction that increasing the realism of the psychological
underpinnings of economic analysis will improve the field of economics on its
own terms.

This conviction does not imply a wholesale rejection of the neoclassical
approach to economics based on utility maximization, equilibrium, and
efficiency. The book assembles the most important papers on behavioral
economics published since around 1990.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. PSYCHOLOGICAL FOUNDATIONS: Behavioral economics incorporates psychological
   realism into economic models

2. NOT ANTI-NEOCLASSICAL: Augments rather than replaces utility maximization,
   equilibrium, and efficiency concepts

3. FIELD EMERGENCE: Documents how behavioral economics went from fringe to
   mainstream (1990-2003)

4. THREE PILLARS: Bounded rationality, bounded willpower, bounded self-interest

5. METHODOLOGY: Combines theoretical models with experimental and field evidence

6. INTEGRATION: Shows how psychological insights can improve standard economic
   predictions
