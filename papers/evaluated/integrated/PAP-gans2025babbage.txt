Title: A Model of the Babbage Firm
Authors: Joshua S. Gans
Year: 2025

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Dynamic model of the COGNITIVE DIVISION OF LABOUR within firms.

SETUP:
- Continuum of questions arrive over time
- Each must be assigned to EXPERT or ROUTINE WORKER

TWO TYPES OF WORKERS:

1. ROUTINE WORKERS: Exploit codified knowledge by INTERPOLATING between
   previously solved problems

2. EXPERTS: Possess TACIT KNOWLEDGE; can either address current question
   or conduct RESEARCH to create new knowledge

MANAGER'S CHOICE:
- Use experts for current issues (EXPLOITATION)
- OR invest in knowledge creation (EXPLORATION)
- Taking into account endogeneity of knowledge base and evolving
  organizational structure

KEY RESULTS:

1. ORGANIZATIONAL TRAJECTORY:
   - Initially: EXPAND by adding distant knowledge points
   - Then: REFINEMENT phase filling gaps between existing anchors
   - Can exhibit S-SHAPED or CONCAVE growth

2. TWO TYPES OF AI WITH OPPOSITE EFFECTS:

   INTERPOLATION AI (improves routine workers' precision):
   → RAISES value of exploration
   → HASTENS routinization

   APPLICATION AI (lowers cost of expert operations):
   → CROWDS OUT research

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Routine workers interpolate; experts have tacit knowledge
2. Organizations: expansion → refinement pattern
3. S-shaped or concave trajectory possible
4. Interpolation-AI: increases exploration, speeds routinization
5. Application-AI: CROWDS OUT research (opposite effect!)
6. Extends Babbage division of labor + March exploration-exploitation
