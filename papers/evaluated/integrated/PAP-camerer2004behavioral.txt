================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Behavioral economics increases the explanatory power of economics by providing
it with more realistic psychological foundations. This introductory chapter
reviews the development of behavioral economics as a field and previews the
representative articles collected in the handbook.

Twenty years ago, behavioral economics did not exist as a field. Most economists
were deeply skeptical—even antagonistic—toward the idea of importing insights
from psychology into their field. Today, behavioral economics has become
virtually mainstream.

While the papers in this book largely adhere to the basic neoclassical framework,
there is nothing inherent in behavioral economics that requires one to embrace
the neoclassical economic model. Indeed, the authors consider it likely that
alternative paradigms will eventually be proposed which have greater explanatory
power.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. DEFINITION: Behavioral economics increases explanatory power through more
   realistic psychological foundations

2. FIELD EMERGENCE: Went from non-existent to mainstream in 20 years
   (1984-2004)

3. NEOCLASSICAL COMPATIBLE: Most behavioral work modifies rather than
   abandons neoclassical framework

4. FUTURE PARADIGMS: Alternative frameworks likely to emerge with greater
   explanatory power

5. INTEGRATION: Psychology and economics can be mutually beneficial

6. EMPIRICAL FOUNDATION: Experimental methods central to behavioral economics
