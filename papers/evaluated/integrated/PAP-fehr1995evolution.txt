ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines the evolutionary foundations of fairness preferences.
Using evolutionary game theory, we show that fairness norms can evolve and
persist in populations through cultural and genetic mechanisms. Strong
reciprocity - the tendency to cooperate and punish defectors - plays a
central role in this evolution.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. EVOLUTIONARY STABILITY
   - Fairness preferences can evolve
   - Cultural transmission mechanisms
   - Gene-culture coevolution

2. STRONG RECIPROCITY
   - Punishment of free-riders
   - Costly enforcement
   - Group selection dynamics

3. PERSISTENCE
   - Fairness norms persist in equilibrium
   - Invasion by selfish types prevented
   - Multiple equilibria possible
