Title: What Does Consulting Do?
Authors: Gert Bijnens, Simon Jäger, Benjamin Schoefer
Year: 2025
Type: NBER Working Paper No. 34072
DOI: 10.3386/w34072

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper provides the FIRST SYSTEMATIC AND COMPREHENSIVE EMPIRICAL STUDY
of management and strategy consulting.

DATA: Universal administrative B2B transaction data based on VALUE-ADDED TAX
links from Belgium (2002-2023).

WHO HIRES CONSULTANTS?
- Concentrated among LARGE, HIGH-LABOR-PRODUCTIVITY firms
- U-SHAPED pattern for TFP and profitability: both HIGH and LOW performers

ENGAGEMENT CHARACTERISTICS:
- Average spend: 3% OF PAYROLL
- Typically EPISODIC engagements lasting LESS THAN ONE YEAR

EFFECTS ON CLIENT FIRMS (Difference-in-Differences):
- Labor productivity: +3.6% over five years
- Driven by modest employment reductions + stable/growing revenue
- Average wages: +2.7%
- NO decline in labor's share of value added

ORGANIZATIONAL CHANGES:
- Small increases in dismissal rates
- Higher services procurement
- REDUCED labor outsourcing

HETEROGENEITY:
LARGER productivity gains for initially LESS PRODUCTIVE firms
→ Suggests improvements in ALLOCATIVE EFFICIENCY

CONCLUSION: Validates PRODUCTIVITY-ENHANCING view of consulting
(endorsed by practitioners) over RENT-SHIFTING view (favored by many economists)

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. First comprehensive empirical study using universal VAT data
2. Consulting spend = 3% of payroll, episodic <1 year
3. +3.6% productivity over 5 years
4. +2.7% wages with stable labor share
5. Larger gains for less productive firms
6. Validates productivity-enhancing over rent-shifting view
