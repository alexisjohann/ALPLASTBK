Title: Out of Control: Visceral Influences on Behavior
Authors: George Loewenstein
Year: 1996
Journal: Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes, Vol. 65, No. 3, pp. 272-292

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Understanding discrepancies between behavior and perceived self-interest has
been a major theoretical challenge in decision theory. People often act against
their self-interest in full knowledge that they are doing so, experiencing a
feeling of being "out of control."

The paper attributes this phenomenon to the operation of "visceral factors,"
which include drive states (hunger, thirst, sexual desire), moods and emotions,
physical pain, and craving for drugs. The defining characteristics of visceral
factors are a direct hedonic impact (usually negative) and an effect on the
relative desirability of different goods and actions.

When in a "hot" visceral state, people behave very differently than when in a
"cold" state, and they systematically underestimate how visceral factors will
affect their future behavior. This "hot-cold empathy gap" explains why people
fail to prepare for temptation, make unrealistic commitments, and later regret
choices made under visceral influence.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. VISCERAL FACTORS: Drive states, emotions, pain, and cravings dominate
   rational calculation

2. HOT-COLD EMPATHY GAP: People in cold states underestimate how hot states
   will affect them

3. STATE-DEPENDENT PREFERENCES: Understanding is "state-dependent" - hard to
   imagine other states

4. COMMITMENT FAILURE: People fail to prepare for future visceral influence

5. REGRET: Choices made in hot states often regretted when visceral factor
   subsides

6. OUT OF CONTROL: Subjective experience of loss of control during visceral
   states
