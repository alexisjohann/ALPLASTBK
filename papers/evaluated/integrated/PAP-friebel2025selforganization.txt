Title: Self-Organization: The Formation of Volunteer Fire Brigades in 19th-Century Baden
Authors: Guido Friebel, Sebastian Koch, Tristan Stahl
Year: 2025
Institution: Goethe University Frankfurt

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

How does SELF-ORGANIZATION emerge?

SETTING: 19th-century Baden (autonomous German state)
- Many but not all communities established VOLUNTEER FIRE BRIGADES
- A form of bottom-up collective action that persists to this day

KEY FINDING:
Self-organization emerged where LOCAL SHOCKS interacted with
PRE-EXISTING SOCIAL STRUCTURES.

THE INTERACTION:
- A major fire significantly increased likelihood of establishing
  a fire brigade
- BUT ONLY in communities that already had a SINGING or GYMNASTICS CLUB

QUANTIFICATION:
Fire x Social Clubs → MORE THAN TRIPLES probability of fire brigade
formation

MECHANISM (Coordination Game Framework):
- Fire ALONE has no effect
- Social clubs ALONE have no effect
- Shocks SHIFT EXPECTATIONS and enable equilibrium transition from
  non-cooperation to cooperation

DATA: Historical panel of 1,577 Baden municipalities (1852-1889)
with hand-collected fire insurance data.

THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTION:
Extends Ostrom (collective action) and Putnam (social capital) theories.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Self-organization = Shock × Pre-existing Social Structure
2. Fire alone: NO EFFECT
3. Social clubs alone: NO EFFECT
4. Fire × Social Clubs: 3× probability of fire brigade
5. Coordination game: shocks enable equilibrium transition
6. Extends Ostrom and Putnam theories
