Title: Migrating Extremists
Authors: Christian Ochsner, Felix Roesel
Year: 2020
Journal: The Economic Journal, Vol. 130, No. 628, pp. 1281-1296
DOI: 10.1093/ej/ueaa017

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

INTERGENERATIONAL VALUE TRANSMISSION of extremist beliefs.

HISTORICAL SETTING:
In 1946, nearly half a million Nazi Party members in Austria faced
DENAZIFICATION policy.

THE NATURAL EXPERIMENT:
- SOVIET occupation zone: Punitive denazification
- US occupation zone: Lenient denazification
→ Many Nazis MIGRATED WESTWARD to US zone

METHODOLOGY - GEONOMASTICS:
Using historical surname analysis (Reichstelefonbuch 1942) to trace
Nazi migration to the present day.

KEY FINDING:
Descendants of Nazi migrants vote disproportionately for the far-right FPÖ.

MECHANISM:
Persistent effect is driven by INTERGENERATIONAL TRANSMISSION OF VALUES.

COMPANION TO OCHSNER/ROESEL 2024:
- 2020 paper: Direct VALUE transmission
- 2024 paper: Latent MEMORY activation

TWO PERSISTENCE PATHWAYS:
1. Values passed directly through generations (this paper)
2. Dormant memories activated by political campaigns (2024)

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Nazi migration 1946 predicts FPÖ voting today
2. Mechanism: intergenerational value transmission
3. Novel method: Geonomastics (surname analysis)
4. Soviet vs US occupation zone as natural experiment
5. Complements activated history research
6. Values persist across generations without activation
