Title: Bitterness in Life and Attitudes Towards Immigration
Authors: Panu Poutvaara, Max Friedrich Steinhardt
Year: 2018
Journal: European Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 55, pp. 471-490
DOI: 10.1016/j.ejpoleco.2018.04.001

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The study shows that bitter people who feel they have not gotten what they
deserve in life worry more about immigration.

This relationship holds for respondents with different levels of:
- Skills
- Job security
- Concerns about crime
- General economic situation
- Own economic situation

Integration of immigrants is a two-way process, the success of which depends
both on immigrants and on natives.

Using data from the 2005 and 2010 waves of the German Socio-Economic Panel,
the authors show that bitterness in life is strongly associated with worries
about immigration, and this effect CANNOT be explained just by concerns that
immigrants are competing with oneself in the labor market.

KEY FINDING: People who feel they have not got what they deserve in life
oppose immigration for SPITEFUL REASONS.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Bitterness predicts anti-immigration attitudes
2. Effect NOT explained by labor market competition concerns
3. Relationship holds across skill levels and job security
4. Bitterness higher among far-right/populist party supporters
5. Spiteful motivations for opposing immigration
6. German SOEP data (2005, 2010)
