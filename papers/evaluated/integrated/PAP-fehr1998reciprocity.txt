================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper surveys the experimental evidence on reciprocal behavior and
discusses its economic implications. Reciprocity means that people reward
kind actions and punish unkind ones. We show that reciprocity has important
implications for contract enforcement, public goods provision, and labor
market outcomes. The presence of reciprocal individuals can fundamentally
change the nature of equilibria.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. HOMO RECIPROCANS
   - Alternative model to Homo Economicus
   - People care about intentions and fairness
   - Willing to sacrifice material payoff to reward/punish

2. CONTRACT ENFORCEMENT
   - Reciprocity enables self-enforcing contracts
   - Workers provide effort above minimum when treated fairly
   - Employers pay efficiency wages anticipating reciprocity

3. PUBLIC GOODS
   - Reciprocity sustains cooperation in repeated interactions
   - Conditional cooperation depends on others' behavior
   - Punishment of free riders by reciprocators

4. LABOR MARKETS
   - Gift exchange: fair wages elicit high effort
   - Wage rigidity explained by reciprocity concerns
   - Unemployment as discipline device less effective
