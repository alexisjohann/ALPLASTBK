Title: Diversification Bias: Explaining the Discrepancy in Variety Seeking Between Combined and Separated Choices
Authors: Daniel Read, George Loewenstein
Year: 1995
Journal: Journal of Experimental Psychology: Applied, Vol. 1, No. 1, pp. 34-49

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The research reveals a diversification bias: if people make combined choices
of quantities of goods for future consumption, they choose more variety than
if they make separate choices immediately preceding consumption.

In experiments, subjects choosing three snacks simultaneously chose a median
of 2.27 different snacks out of 3, while those choosing sequentially chose
only 1.61 different snacks - about 40% more variety in simultaneous choice.
Sequential choosers got more of their favorite snacks.

The diversification bias is largely attributable to two mechanisms:
1. TIME CONTRACTION: tendency to compress time intervals and treat long
   intervals as if they were short (expecting boredom that won't occur)
2. CHOICE BRACKETING: tendency to treat choices framed together differently
   from those framed apart (seeking variety within the bracket)

Traditional utility maximization cannot explain the discrepancy between
combined and separate choices - it reflects systematic prediction error about
future preferences.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. DIVERSIFICATION BIAS: Simultaneous choice yields ~40% more variety than
   sequential

2. TIME CONTRACTION: People underestimate time between consumption occasions

3. CHOICE BRACKETING: Choices framed together treated as needing variety

4. PREDICTION ERROR: People mispredict desire for variety

5. SEQUENTIAL BETTER: Sequential choosers get more of favorites, higher
   satisfaction

6. FRAMING EFFECT: How choices are grouped affects variety seeking
