================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how contracts serve as reference points that shape
behavioral responses. Building on Köszegi-Rabin reference-dependent preferences,
we show that contracts establish expectations that affect how people respond
to outcomes. Competition affects contract design through its impact on
reference points.
