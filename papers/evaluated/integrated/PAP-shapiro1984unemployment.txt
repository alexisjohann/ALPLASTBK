Title: Equilibrium Unemployment as a Worker Discipline Device
Authors: Carl Shapiro, Joseph E. Stiglitz
Year: 1984
Journal: American Economic Review, Vol. 74, No. 3, pp. 433-444

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper shows how the information structure of employer-employee
relationships, in particular the inability of employers to costlessly observe
workers' on-the-job effort, can explain involuntary unemployment as an
equilibrium phenomenon. Indeed, imperfect monitoring necessitates unemployment
in equilibrium.

The key insight is that with imperfect monitoring and full employment, workers
would choose to shirk because the worst that could happen is being fired and
immediately rehired elsewhere. Unemployment serves as a worker discipline
device by making job loss costly. In equilibrium, all firms pay wages above
market-clearing levels, creating unemployment that provides the penalty
necessary to deter shirking.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. UNEMPLOYMENT AS DISCIPLINE: Equilibrium unemployment makes job loss costly,
   providing incentive for workers not to shirk

2. EFFICIENCY WAGES: All firms pay above market-clearing wages to induce
   effort; no firm can profitably cut wages because workers would shirk

3. IMPERFECT MONITORING: The model requires that employers cannot costlessly
   observe worker effort - if monitoring were free, no unemployment needed

4. NO-SHIRKING CONDITION (NSC): Workers must be paid enough that the expected
   utility of not shirking exceeds the utility of shirking

5. AGGREGATE DEMAND EFFECTS: Factors that increase aggregate labor demand
   (like government spending) can actually increase unemployment

6. POLICY IMPLICATIONS: Monitoring costs, unemployment benefits, and labor
   market structure all affect equilibrium unemployment rate
