Title: Wage Rigidities and Job Creation: Theory and Evidence from Adjustment Costs
Authors: Ernst Fehr
Journal: International Economic Review
Year: 2005

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines wage rigidities and their impact on job creation. We
analyze how adjustment costs and fairness concerns contribute to wage
stickiness and affect employment dynamics.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. WAGE RIGIDITY SOURCES
   - Fairness norms create downward rigidity
   - Adjustment costs compound stickiness
   - Worker reference points matter

2. JOB CREATION DYNAMICS
   - Rigid wages affect hiring decisions
   - Labor market clearing impaired
   - Unemployment persistence

3. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Labor market institutions matter
   - Flexibility vs. fairness trade-off
   - Adjustment cost reduction
