================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how wealth affects distributional preferences. We show
that wealth can affect both the intensity and direction of social preferences.
Understanding this relationship is crucial for predicting how economic growth
and inequality affect social cohesion and policy preferences.
