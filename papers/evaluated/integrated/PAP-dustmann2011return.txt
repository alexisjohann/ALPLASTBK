Title: Return Migration, Investment in Children, and Intergenerational Mobility:
       Comparing Sons of Foreign- and Native-Born Fathers
Authors: Christian Dustmann
Year: 2008 (IZA DP 2007, Journal 2008)
Journal: Journal of Human Resources, Vol. 43, No. 2, pp. 299-324
DOI: 10.3368/jhr.43.2.299

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper studies parental investment in education and intergenerational
earnings mobility for father-son pairs with native- and foreign-born fathers.
The author illustrates within a simple model that for immigrants, investment
in their children is related to their return migration probability.

In the empirical analysis, a measure for return probabilities is included,
based on repeated information about migrants' return intentions.

The results suggest that educational investments in the son are positively
associated with a higher probability of a permanent migration of the father.
The study also finds that the son's permanent wages are positively associated
with the probability of the father's permanent migration.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Return migration intentions affect parental investment in children
2. Permanent migration → higher educational investment in children
3. Son's wages positively correlated with father's permanent migration probability
4. Model: Investment decisions linked to expected location of returns
5. Data: Repeated surveys capturing changing return intentions
