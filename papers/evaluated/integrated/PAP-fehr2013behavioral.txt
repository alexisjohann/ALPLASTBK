================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how behavioral insights can improve policy design.
We review evidence on how cognitive biases, social preferences, and limited
attention affect policy responses. We discuss choice architecture, nudges,
and other behavioral interventions that can enhance policy effectiveness.
