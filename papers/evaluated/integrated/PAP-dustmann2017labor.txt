Title: Expansions in Maternity Leave Coverage and Children's Long-Term Outcomes
Authors: Christian Dustmann, Uta Schönberg
Year: 2012 (originally dated as 2017 in queue - corrected)
Journal: American Economic Journal: Applied Economics, Vol. 4, No. 3, pp. 190-224
DOI: 10.1257/app.4.3.190

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper evaluates the impact of three major expansions in maternity leave
coverage in Germany on children's long-run outcomes. To identify the causal
impact of the reforms, the authors use a difference-in-difference design that
compares outcomes of children born shortly before and shortly after a change
in maternity leave legislation in years of policy changes, and in years when
no changes have taken place.

The authors find no support for the hypothesis that the expansions in leave
coverage improved children's outcomes, despite a strong impact on mothers'
return to work behavior after childbirth.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Three major German maternity leave expansions studied
2. Methodology: Difference-in-differences comparing children born before/after reforms
3. Strong effect on mothers' return-to-work timing
4. NO significant improvement in children's long-term outcomes
5. Challenges assumption that longer leave = better child outcomes
6. JEL Classifications: J13, J16, J22, J32

================================================================================
METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTIONS
================================================================================

- Identifies causal impact using policy reform timing (RD-like design)
- Evaluates series of policy changes within same country
- Focuses on LONG-TERM effects (not just immediate outcomes)
- Weaker identification assumptions than existing studies
