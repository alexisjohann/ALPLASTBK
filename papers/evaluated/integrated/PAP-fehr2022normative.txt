ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This review examines the relationship between social norms, cooperation,
and inequality. We discuss how social norms shape economic behavior and
how inequality affects norm compliance and enforcement. Understanding
this interplay is crucial for designing policies that promote cooperation
and reduce harmful inequality.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. NORMS AND COOPERATION
   - Cooperation norms vary across societies
   - Norm strength predicts cooperation levels
   - Enforcement mechanisms differ culturally

2. INEQUALITY AND NORMS
   - High inequality weakens cooperation norms
   - Perceptions of unfairness reduce compliance
   - Elite behavior affects norm credibility

3. DYNAMIC INTERACTIONS
   - Norms affect inequality outcomes
   - Inequality feeds back to norms
   - Potential for vicious/virtuous cycles

4. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Design institutions that reinforce good norms
   - Address inequality to sustain cooperation
   - Consider norm dynamics in policy evaluation
