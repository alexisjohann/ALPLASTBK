Title: Labor Supply Shocks, Native Wages, and the Adjustment of Local Employment
Authors: Christian Dustmann, Uta Schönberg, Jan Stuhler
Year: 2017
Journal: Quarterly Journal of Economics, Vol. 132, No. 1, pp. 435-483
DOI: 10.1093/qje/qjw032

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

By exploiting a commuting policy that led to a sharp and unexpected inflow of
Czech workers to areas along the German-Czech border, the authors examine the
impact of an exogenous immigration-induced labor supply shock on local wages
and employment of natives.

KEY FINDINGS:

On average, the supply shock leads to a MODERATE DECLINE in local native wages
and a SHARP DECLINE in local native employment.

These average effects mask considerable HETEROGENEITY across groups:
- Younger natives: LARGER wage effects
- Older natives: Particularly pronounced EMPLOYMENT responses

This pattern is INCONSISTENT with standard models of immigration but can be
accounted for by a model that allows for:
- Larger labor supply elasticity for older workers, OR
- Higher degree of wage rigidity for older workers

The employment response is almost entirely driven by DIMINISHED INFLOWS of
natives into work rather than outflows, suggesting that "OUTSIDERS" shield
"INSIDERS" from the effects of the labor supply shock.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Natural experiment: Czech commuter policy (sharp, unexpected shock)
2. Moderate wage decline, sharp employment decline for natives
3. Age heterogeneity: Young = wage effects, Old = employment effects
4. Standard immigration models fail to explain patterns
5. "Outsiders shield insiders" mechanism
6. Inflow effects dominate outflow effects

================================================================================
METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTION
================================================================================

- Quasi-experimental design (commuting policy change)
- Geographic regression discontinuity
- Age-specific effects estimation
- Insider-outsider framework application
- German-Czech border as natural experiment
