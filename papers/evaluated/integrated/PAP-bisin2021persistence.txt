================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Is long-term economic PERSISTENCE a myth?

This survey examines what has been learned from the wave of articles
examining the persistence of economic phenomena.

CONTRIBUTIONS:
1. Clarifies CONCEPTS of persistence
2. Distinguishes different STRANDS of the literature
3. Questions validity of recent CRITIQUES of persistence papers

CHALLENGES ACKNOWLEDGED:
- MISATTRIBUTION: Wrong causal attribution
- P-HACKING: Selective reporting of results

CENTRAL CONCLUSION:
PERSISTENCE IS REAL.

While challenges abound, the survey concludes that persistence effects
are genuine and empirically robust.

RESEARCH AGENDA:
Future research should focus on:
1. MECHANISMS: How does persistence work?
2. BREAKDOWN CONDITIONS: When does persistence fail?

RELEVANCE FOR BEHAVIORAL ECONOMICS:
Historical context has long-term effects on current behavior.
Supports the EBF Ψ-framework: context matters across generations.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS

1. Persistence is REAL (not a myth)
2. Challenges: misattribution, p-hacking acknowledged

5. Focus needed on BREAKDOWN CONDITIONS
