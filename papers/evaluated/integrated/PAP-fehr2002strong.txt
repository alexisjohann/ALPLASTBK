Title: Strong Reciprocity, Human Cooperation, and the Enforcement of Social Norms

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Strong reciprocity is a behavioral disposition to cooperate conditionally on
others' cooperation and to punish non-cooperators, even if punishment is
costly and yields no material benefit for the punisher. We present evidence
from economic experiments showing that strong reciprocity is a powerful force
for the enforcement of social norms and the maintenance of cooperation.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. STRONG RECIPROCITY DEFINED
   - Cooperate if others cooperate (positive reciprocity)
   - Punish defectors even at personal cost (negative reciprocity)
   - Not explained by reputation or repeated game incentives
   - Operates even in anonymous one-shot interactions

2. NORM ENFORCEMENT
   - Strong reciprocators enforce social norms
   - Punishment deters free riding
   - Creates cooperation even among selfish individuals
   - Second-order free-rider problem mitigated

3. EVOLUTIONARY PERSPECTIVE
   - Strong reciprocity may have evolved through group selection
   - Culturally stable equilibrium
   - Coevolution of cooperation and punishment norms

4. EXPERIMENTAL EVIDENCE
   - Public goods games with punishment
   - Third-party punishment experiments
   - Cross-cultural evidence of strong reciprocity
