ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This EU Parliament policy study examines the influence of social media on
the development of children and young people, using the 4Cs RISK FRAMEWORK:

1. CONTENT RISKS: Exposure to harmful, illegal, or age-inappropriate content
2. CONTACT RISKS: Unwanted contact from adults or strangers
3. CONDUCT RISKS: Children's own problematic behavior online
4. CONTRACT RISKS: Commercial exploitation, data misuse

POLICY FRAMEWORKS REVIEWED:
- Better Internet for Kids+ (BIK+)
- Digital Services Act (DSA)
- Audiovisual Media Services Directive (AVMSD)
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- Age Appropriate Design Codes (international)

FIVE POLICY RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Strengthen age verification mechanisms
2. Enhance transparency of algorithmic systems
3. Improve digital literacy education
4. Establish clearer platform accountability
5. Develop child-centered design standards
