Title: Activated History: The Case of the Turkish Sieges of Vienna
Authors: Christian Ochsner, Felix Roesel
Year: 2024
Journal: American Economic Journal: Applied Economics, Vol. 16, No. 3, pp. 76-112
DOI: 10.1257/app.20190686

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

CRUCIAL EXTENSION to persistence literature.

KEY INSIGHT:
Historical persistence can be LATENT - dormant for centuries until
ACTIVATED by political campaigns.

HISTORICAL SETTING:
Turkish troops besieged Vienna in 1529 and 1683, pillaging individual
Austrian villages, killing and kidnapping.

FOR CENTURIES:
Attacked places remember events well BUT never expressed aversion to
Muslims.

THE ACTIVATION:
Far-right populists (FPÖ) started campaigning against Turks and Muslims
in the mid-2000s.

RESULTS:
After populist campaigns were launched:
- Anti-Muslim sentiments SURGE in previously attacked places
- Far-right voting INCREASES in pillaged villages
- Turkish communities DECREASE in response (out-migration)

MECHANISM:
COLLECTIVE MEMORY + POLITICAL ACTIVATION = RADICALIZATION

History × Campaign interaction

COMPLEMENT TO VOTH TRILOGY:
- Voth shows CONTINUOUS persistence
- Ochsner/Roesel show ACTIVATED persistence

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Persistence can be LATENT until activated
2. Turkish sieges (1529/1683) had no effect for centuries
3. FPÖ campaign (2000s) activated dormant memories
4. Pillaged villages became FPÖ strongholds
5. Turkish communities left in response
6. History × Campaign interaction is key
