Title: When Social Norms Overpower Competition
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization
Year: 2003

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines conditions under which social norms overpower competitive
pressures. We show that even in highly competitive markets, social norms of
fairness can prevent market clearing and sustain non-competitive outcomes.
The strength of norms depends on the social context and institutional design.

KEY FINDINGS

1. NORMS VS COMPETITION
   - Norms can override competition

2. CONDITIONS FOR NORM DOMINANCE
   - Repeated interactions help

   - Punishment opportunities key

3. INSTITUTIONAL FACTORS
   - Transparency affects norms
