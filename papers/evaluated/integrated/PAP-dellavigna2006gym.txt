Title: Paying Not to Go to the Gym
Authors: Stefano DellaVigna, Ulrike Malmendier
Year: 2006
Journal: American Economic Review, Vol. 96, No. 3, pp. 694-719

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Health club members who choose a contract with a flat monthly fee of over $70
pay per average attendance more than $17 per visit even though a $10-per-visit
fee is available. On average, members who choose the monthly contract attend
4.17 times per month, paying $17.27 per visit. Members who choose the annual
contract pay about $15 per visit.

The exposed preference for the flat-rate contract is not explained by risk
aversion, transaction costs, or price uncertainty. The evidence is consistent
with overconfidence about future self-control: consumers overestimate their
future attendance when they sign up. Average predicted attendance at sign-up
is 9.5 visits per month vs. 4.2 actual.

Further evidence: members delay cancellation of memberships for an average of
2.31 months, paying $187 in extra fees. This behavior is consistent with
limited attention and present-biased preferences.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. OVERCONFIDENCE: Gym members overestimate attendance by 2.3x (9.5 predicted
   vs 4.2 actual visits per month)

2. FLAT-RATE BIAS: Members pay $17.27/visit with monthly plan when $10/visit
   available - a 73% premium

3. CANCELLATION DELAY: Members wait 2.31 months longer than optimal to cancel,
   paying $187 in unnecessary fees

4. NOT RISK AVERSION: Pattern cannot be explained by rational risk preferences
   or transaction costs

5. PRESENT BIAS: Consistent with β-δ preferences - immediate costs of
   cancellation vs delayed benefits

6. NAIVE BELIEFS: Consumers are naive about their own present bias, expecting
   future self to exercise more
