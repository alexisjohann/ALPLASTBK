ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This Annual Review article examines the behavioral economics of work. We
review evidence on how fairness, reciprocity, identity, and social comparisons
affect worker behavior. These factors have important implications for HR
practices, compensation design, and organizational performance.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. FAIRNESS AT WORK
   - Wage fairness matters
   - Effort responds to treatment
   - Gift exchange in action

2. SOCIAL COMPARISONS
   - Relative pay matters
   - Peer effects significant
   - Inequity aversion

3. IDENTITY AND WORK
   - Work shapes identity
   - Identity affects effort
   - Organizational culture

4. HR IMPLICATIONS
   - Compensation design
   - Management practices
   - Organizational culture
