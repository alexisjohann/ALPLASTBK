Title: Mental Accounting and Consumer Choice
Authors: Richard H. Thaler
Year: 1985
Journal: Marketing Science, Vol. 4, No. 3, pp. 199-214
DOI: 10.1287/mksc.4.3.199

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

A new model of consumer behavior is developed using a hybrid of cognitive
psychology and microeconomics. The development of the model starts with the
mental coding of combinations of gains and losses using the prospect theory
value function.

Then the evaluation of purchases is modeled using the new concept of
"transaction utility." The household budgeting process is also incorporated
to complete the characterization of mental accounting.

Several implications to marketing, particularly in the area of pricing, are
developed.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. MENTAL ACCOUNTING: People categorize money into different mental accounts
   (food, entertainment, savings) rather than treating it as fungible

2. TRANSACTION UTILITY: Utility from a purchase has two components:
   - Acquisition utility (value vs price)
   - Transaction utility (deal vs reference price)

3. PROSPECT THEORY APPLICATION: Mental coding follows prospect theory's
   value function for gains and losses

4. SEGREGATION PRINCIPLE: Multiple gains should be segregated, multiple
   losses should be integrated for maximum satisfaction

5. HOUSEHOLD BUDGETING: Mental accounts constrain spending categories

6. MARKETING IMPLICATIONS: Pricing strategies should account for reference
   prices and transaction utility
