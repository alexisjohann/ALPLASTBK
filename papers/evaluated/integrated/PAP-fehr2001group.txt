================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This handbook chapter examines how group formation affects cooperation.
We review evidence on how endogenous group formation, group identity, and
group competition affect cooperative behavior. Understanding these dynamics
is important for organizational design and team formation.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. ENDOGENOUS GROUPS
   - Self-selection matters
   - Sorting by type occurs
   - Better outcomes possible

2. GROUP IDENTITY
   - In-group favoritism
   - Cooperation with in-group
   - Out-group discrimination

3. INTER-GROUP COMPETITION
   - Increases in-group cooperation
   - Can reduce overall welfare
   - Design considerations
