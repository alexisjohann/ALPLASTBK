Title: The Evolution of Cooperation and Punishment
Authors: Ernst Fehr
Journal: Proceedings of the Royal Society B
Year: 1999

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines the evolutionary foundations of cooperation and punishment
in human societies. We analyze how costly punishment can evolve and sustain
cooperation even in large groups where reputation effects are limited.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. EVOLUTIONARY DYNAMICS
   - Punishment stabilizes cooperation
   - Second-order free-rider problem
   - Group selection mechanisms

2. COOPERATION MECHANISMS
   - Altruistic punishment
   - Indirect reciprocity
   - Reputation effects

3. LARGE GROUP COOPERATION
   - Punishment overcomes group size limits
   - Institutional enforcement evolves
   - Cultural transmission matters
