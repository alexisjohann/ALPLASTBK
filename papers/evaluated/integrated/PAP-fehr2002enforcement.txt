Title: Enforcement Problems and the Evolution of Institutions
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Journal of Economic Perspectives
Year: 2002

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how enforcement problems shape the evolution of economic
institutions. We argue that strong reciprocity - the willingness to punish
norm violators at personal cost - is crucial for understanding how informal
institutions enforce cooperation. The presence of strong reciprocators
enables self-enforcing contracts and sustains social norms.

KEY FINDINGS

1. ENFORCEMENT PROBLEMS
   - Contracts are incomplete
   - Formal enforcement costly

2. STRONG RECIPROCITY
   - Punishment of violators
   - Costly but effective
   - Decentralized enforcement

3. INSTITUTIONAL EVOLUTION
   - Institutions embed norms
   - Cultural evolution shapes institutions
   - Complementarity between formal/informal

4. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Work with existing norms
   - Crowding effects of formal rules
   - Design for self-enforcement
