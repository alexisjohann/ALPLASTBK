ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This JEL review surveys two decades of behavioral economics research.
We summarize major findings, assess the field's progress, and identify
remaining challenges and future directions.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. MAJOR ADVANCES
   - Social preferences established
   - Bounded rationality confirmed
   - Field experiments expanded

2. METHODOLOGICAL PROGRESS
   - Experimental methods refined
   - Neuroimaging integrated
   - Structural estimation

3. REMAINING CHALLENGES
   - External validity
   - Welfare analysis
   - Policy implementation
