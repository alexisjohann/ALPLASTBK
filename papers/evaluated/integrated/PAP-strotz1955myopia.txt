Title: Myopia and Inconsistency in Dynamic Utility Maximization
Authors: Robert H. Strotz
Year: 1955
Journal: Review of Economic Studies, Vol. 23, No. 3, pp. 165-180

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The paper presents a problem which had not previously been analyzed, and provides
a theory to explain, under different circumstances, three related phenomena:
(1) spendthriftiness; (2) the deliberate regimenting of one's future economic
behavior—even at a cost; and (3) thrift.

Strotz was among the first to study the behavior of an economic agent whose
preferences change over time. He suggested that such an agent would choose a
"consistent plan" - anticipating future preference changes and accounting for
them in current decisions.

This foundational paper laid the groundwork for modern theories of
time-inconsistent preferences, hyperbolic discounting, and commitment devices.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. DYNAMIC INCONSISTENCY: Preferences change over time in predictable ways

2. PRECOMMITMENT: People deliberately constrain future behavior to avoid
   temptation

3. SPENDTHRIFTINESS: Results from inability to commit to future savings plans

4. THRIFT: Achieved through commitment devices that restrict future choices

5. CONSISTENT PLANNING: Sophisticated agents anticipate preference changes

6. FOUNDATIONAL: First formal treatment of time-inconsistent preferences
