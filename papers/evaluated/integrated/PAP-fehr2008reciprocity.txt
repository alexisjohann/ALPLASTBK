Title: Who Behaves Selfishly, and Why? Evidence from Salaried and Piece-Rate Workers
Authors: Ernst Fehr
Journal: Econometrica
Year: 2008

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines who behaves selfishly and why, using evidence from workers
under different compensation schemes. We compare salaried and piece-rate workers
to understand how payment structure affects reciprocal behavior.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. COMPENSATION EFFECTS
   - Piece-rate reduces reciprocity
   - Salaried workers more cooperative
   - Selection and treatment effects

2. INDIVIDUAL DIFFERENCES
   - Type heterogeneity
   - Selfish vs. reciprocal types
   - Context-dependent behavior

3. WORKPLACE IMPLICATIONS
   - Compensation design matters
   - Team production considerations
   - Organizational culture
