Title: Gift Exchange and Reciprocity in Competitive Experimental Markets
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Georg Kirchsteiger, Arno Riedl
Year: 1998
Journal: European Economic Review, Vol. 42, No. 1, pp. 1-34
Note: Earlier version as IHS Economics Series Working Paper No. 14 (1995)

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper reports the results of an experiment designed to test the impact of
fairness and reciprocity on market prices. Prices were determined in a one-sided
oral auction, with buyers as price-makers.

The results show that workers' reciprocal behavior increases effort and, hence,
the efficiency of trades. Many people have a tendency to voluntarily cooperate,
if treated fairly, and to punish noncooperators - a behavioral propensity called
"strong reciprocity" that can lead to almost universal cooperation in
circumstances where purely self-interested behavior would cause a complete
breakdown of cooperation.

The experimental evidence demonstrates that fairness concerns have significant
effects on market outcomes even in competitive settings. Gift exchange between
firms and workers emerges endogenously: firms pay wages above the market-clearing
level and workers reciprocate with effort above the minimum.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. GIFT EXCHANGE: Firms voluntarily pay high wages, workers reciprocate with
   high effort

2. EFFICIENCY GAINS: Reciprocity increases trade efficiency beyond self-interest
   predictions

3. MARKET PRICES: Fairness concerns affect price formation even in competitive
   markets

4. STRONG RECIPROCITY: Tendency to cooperate when treated fairly, punish
   when treated unfairly

5. ABOVE-MINIMUM EFFORT: Workers provide effort exceeding contracted minimum

6. NON-CLEARING WAGES: Wages remain above market-clearing level due to fairness
