Title: Does Fairness Prevent Market Clearing? An Experimental Investigation
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Georg Kirchsteiger, Arno Riedl

Year: 1993

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper provides experimental evidence that fairness considerations prevent
labor markets from clearing. In competitive experimental markets, we observe
that firms offer wages substantially above market-clearing levels and workers
reciprocate with higher effort. This behavior is consistent with gift exchange
and efficiency wage theory.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. ABOVE-CLEARING WAGES
   - Firms voluntarily pay premium wages
   - Premium persists despite competition
   - Workers respond to perceived fairness

2. EFFORT RECIPROCITY
   - High wages elicit high effort
   - Low wages get minimum effort
   - Clear gift exchange pattern

3. MARKET INEFFICIENCY
   - Markets do not clear
   - Voluntary unemployment
   - Stable inefficient equilibrium

4. IMPLICATIONS
   - Efficiency wages empirically supported
   - Standard competitive theory incomplete
   - Fairness matters for market outcomes
