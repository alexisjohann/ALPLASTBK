Title: The Economics of Social Media
Authors: Guy Aridor, Rafael Jiménez-Durán, Ro'ee Levy, Lena Song
Year: 2024
Journal: Journal of Economic Literature

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper provides a GUIDE to the burgeoning literature on the economics of
social media. The authors first define social media platforms and highlight
their unique features.

The paper synthesizes the main lessons from the empirical economics literature
and organizes them around the THREE STAGES of the life cycle of content:

1. PRODUCTION:
   - How incentives affect content produced on and off social media
   - How harmful content is moderated

2. DISTRIBUTION:
   - Social network structure
   - Algorithms
   - Targeted advertisements

3. CONSUMPTION:
   - How social media affects individuals who consume its content
   - Effects on society at large
   - Consumer substitution patterns across platforms

CASE STUDIES: Deterrence of misinformation, segregation, political
advertisements, and the effects of social media on political outcomes.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Framework: Production → Distribution → Consumption lifecycle
2. Algorithmic distribution shapes information exposure
3. Moderation policies affect content ecosystem
4. Political outcomes influenced by social media
5. Misinformation and segregation as key concerns
6. Platform competition and substitution patterns
