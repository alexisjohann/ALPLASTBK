================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This handbook chapter examines the effectiveness of trust-based management.
We review experimental evidence on how trust-based approaches compare to
control-based management, and identify conditions under which trust-based
management is more effective.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. TRUST VS CONTROL
   - Trust often more effective
   - Control can backfire
   - Signaling effects matter

2. CONDITIONS FOR TRUST
   - Repeated interactions help
   - Selection possible
   - Reputation systems

3. PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS
   - Balance trust and verification
   - Build trustworthy culture
   - Select for trustworthiness
