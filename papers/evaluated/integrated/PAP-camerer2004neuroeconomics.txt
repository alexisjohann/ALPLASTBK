================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

NEUROECONOMICS uses knowledge about BRAIN MECHANISMS to inform economic
analysis.

BRAIN STRUCTURE:
The brain is composed of MULTIPLE SYSTEMS which interact:
- CONTROLLED SYSTEMS (executive function) interrupt automatic ones
- EMOTIONS and COGNITION both guide decisions

NEUROSCIENTIFIC TOOLS:
- Brain imaging (fMRI, PET)
- Behavior of patients with localized brain lesions
- Animal behavior studies
- Recording single neuron activity

KEY INSIGHT FOR ECONOMICS:
DUAL-PROCESS MODELS are:
- Better rooted in neuroscientific fact
- More empirically accurate
than SINGLE-PROCESS MODELS such as utility-maximization.

ANALOGY:
This opens up the BLACK BOX of the brain, much as organizational
economics adds detail to the theory of the firm.

IMPLICATIONS:
- System 1 (automatic) vs System 2 (controlled) distinction has neural basis
- Emotions are integral to decision-making, not noise
- Multiple brain systems can conflict, explaining inconsistent choices

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Brain has MULTIPLE interacting systems
2. Controlled (executive) systems interrupt automatic ones
3. Emotions and cognition BOTH guide decisions
4. Dual-process models supported by neuroscience
5. Single-process utility maximization less accurate
6. Foundation paper for neuroeconomics field
