Title: Reciprocity and Economics: The Economic Implications of Homo Reciprocans
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter, Georg Kirchsteiger
Journal: Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization
Year: 1997

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper develops the concept of Homo Reciprocans as an alternative to
Homo Economicus. Reciprocal individuals reward kindness and punish unkindness,
even at personal cost. We show that the presence of reciprocal individuals
fundamentally changes economic predictions about contracts, public goods,
and labor markets.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. HOMO RECIPROCANS
   - Rewards kind actions
   - Punishes unkind actions
   - Sacrifices material payoffs

2. CONTRACT IMPLICATIONS
   - Self-enforcing contracts possible
   - Incomplete contracts work better
   - Trust enables cooperation

3. PUBLIC GOODS
   - Reciprocators sustain provision
   - Punishment of free-riders
   - Conditional cooperation

4. LABOR MARKETS
   - Gift exchange equilibrium
   - Efficiency wages emerge
   - Unemployment persists
