Title: Reciprocal Fairness and Group Formation
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Bettina Rockenbach
Journal: Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization
Year: 2013

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how reciprocal fairness affects group formation.
We analyze how fairness considerations influence who people choose to
interact with and how groups emerge based on shared fairness norms.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. GROUP FORMATION
   - Fairness preferences drive sorting
   - Like attracts like
   - Homogeneous groups form

2. RECIPROCITY IN GROUPS
   - In-group reciprocity stronger
   - Out-group treatment differs
   - Identity effects

3. IMPLICATIONS
   - Social segregation
   - Cooperation patterns
   - Institutional design
