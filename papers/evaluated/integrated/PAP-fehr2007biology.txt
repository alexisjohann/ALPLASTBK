ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper reviews the biological foundations of trust and cooperation.
Drawing on neuroscience, genetics, and evolutionary biology, we examine
how biological factors shape human social behavior. Oxytocin, brain reward
systems, and genetic variation all play roles in individual differences
in trust and cooperation.
