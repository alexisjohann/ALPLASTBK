Title: The Effects of Human Resource Management Practices on Productivity:
       A Study of Steel Finishing Lines
Authors: Casey Ichniowski, Kathryn Shaw, Giovanna Prennushi
Year: 1997
Journal: American Economic Review, Vol. 87, No. 3, pp. 291-313

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper investigates the productivity effects of innovative employment
practices using detailed longitudinal data from a sample of thirty-six
homogeneous steel production lines owned by seventeen companies.

The productivity regressions demonstrate that lines using a set of innovative
work practices—including incentive pay, teams, flexible job assignments,
employment security, and training—achieve substantially higher levels of
productivity than do lines with the more traditional approach, which includes
narrow job definitions, strict work rules, and hourly pay with close
supervision.

Critically, the results show that complementarities among work practices are
essential: adopting individual practices in isolation has little effect, but
adopting coherent systems of practices yields large productivity gains.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. SYSTEMS OF PRACTICES MATTER: Coherent systems of innovative HRM practices
   increase productivity by 7% more than adopting individual practices

2. COMPLEMENTARITY: Individual practices have small effects; the interaction
   effects among practices are large and positive

3. INNOVATIVE PRACTICES BUNDLE: High-performance systems include incentive pay,
   teams, flexible job assignments, employment security, and training

4. TRADITIONAL PRACTICES BUNDLE: Low-performance systems feature narrow job
   definitions, strict work rules, hourly pay, and close supervision

5. WITHIN-LINE VARIATION: Results robust to line fixed effects, ruling out
   unobserved heterogeneity explanations

6. LARGE MAGNITUDE: Lines with full innovative systems achieve 7-10% higher
   productivity than traditional lines
