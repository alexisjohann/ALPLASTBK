Title: Norm Enforcement with Decentralized Sanctioning
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization
Year: 1996

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper studies how social norms are enforced through decentralized
sanctioning. We show that individuals willingly bear costs to punish norm
violators, even when they cannot benefit from the punishment directly.
This altruistic punishment is key to understanding norm enforcement in
large groups.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. DECENTRALIZED PUNISHMENT
   - Individuals punish at personal cost
   - No direct benefit to punisher
   - Sustains cooperation in groups

2. ALTRUISTIC PUNISHMENT
   - Punishers are strong reciprocators
   - Emotion-driven responses
   - Effective deterrence

3. NORM STABILITY
   - Norms persist with punishment
   - Violation rates decline
   - Self-enforcing equilibrium
