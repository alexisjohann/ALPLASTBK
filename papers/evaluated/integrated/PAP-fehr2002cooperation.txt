================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how cooperation can be sustained through enforcement of
high efficiency standards. In experimental labor markets, we show that
workers respond to fair wages with high effort, and employers anticipate
this by paying above market-clearing wages. The threat of termination and
reputation concerns help sustain this cooperative equilibrium.

1. HIGH EFFICIENCY EQUILIBRIUM

2. ENFORCEMENT MECHANISMS

   - Reputation concerns

3. IMPLICATIONS FOR LABOR MARKETS
   - Explains wage rigidity
   - Efficiency wage theory supported
   - Fairness matters empirically
