================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines voting behavior on redistribution policies. We analyze
how social preferences, particularly inequality aversion, affect support
for redistributive taxation and transfer programs.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. REDISTRIBUTIVE PREFERENCES
   - Inequality aversion drives support
   - Beyond self-interest voting
   - Fairness concerns matter

2. VOTING BEHAVIOR
   - Rich may support redistribution
   - Poor may oppose transfers
   - Social mobility expectations

3. POLICY DESIGN IMPLICATIONS
   - Framing affects support
   - Perceived fairness crucial
   - Targeting mechanisms matter

support patterns.
