Title: Third-Party Punishment and Norm Enforcement
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Journal of Experimental Social Psychology
Year: 2006

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines third-party punishment as a mechanism for norm enforcement.
We show that uninvolved observers willingly punish norm violators at personal
cost, extending previous findings. Third-party punishment is essential for
understanding large-scale norm enforcement.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. THIRD-PARTY PUNISHMENT
   - Observers punish violators
   - Costly to punisher
   - Not direct beneficiaries

2. NORM ENFORCEMENT
   - Enables large-scale norms
   - Decentralized mechanism
   - Multiplier effect

3. PSYCHOLOGICAL MECHANISMS
   - Moral outrage
   - Justice concerns
   - Emotional responses
