Title: Consumer Demand and Market Competition with Time-Intensive Goods
Authors: Joseph Goodman, Lancelot Henry de Frahan, Justin E. Holz, John A. List,
         Niall MacMenamin, Evan C. McKay, Magne Mogstad, Sally Sadoff, Hal Sider
Year: 2026
Type: NBER Working Paper No. 34743
DOI: 10.3386/w34743

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Application of BECKER'S TIME ALLOCATION THEORY to examine consumer demand
and market competition for TIME-INTENSIVE GOODS.

BECKERIAN MODEL PREDICTIONS:
- Higher DIVERSION RATIOS for goods with substantial time shares
- Higher diversion for goods with high time costs relative to monetary prices

DATA: Two field experiments analyzing demand for Facebook and Instagram

KEY FINDINGS:

1. LOW ELASTICITY TO AD LOAD
   Users exhibit low elasticity to ad load (the primary user cost)

2. TIME SHARES MATTER
   Time shares and time costs significantly influence diversion ratios

3. SUBSTITUTION PATTERNS
   Analysis of substitution across online activities and offline time use

POLICY IMPLICATIONS:
Assessment of potential impact of FTC-proposed DE-MERGER of Facebook
and Instagram.

THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTION:
Connects Becker time allocation (MS-TA-001) to digital platform economics
and attention economy (MS-TA-003).
