ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This handbook chapter reviews how people make moral decisions from a
behavioral economics perspective. We examine the psychological and neural
mechanisms underlying moral judgment, the role of emotions in ethical
decisions, and how context affects moral behavior. Understanding behavioral
ethics is crucial for policy design.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. MORAL DECISION-MAKING
   - Not purely rational process
   - Emotions play key role
   - Context strongly matters

2. PSYCHOLOGICAL MECHANISMS
   - Dual-process theories
   - Intuition vs deliberation
   - Moral disgust and anger

3. CONTEXT EFFECTS
   - Framing affects moral choices
   - Social pressure influences behavior
   - Anonymity changes decisions

4. IMPLICATIONS
   - Ethics cannot be assumed
   - Design matters for moral behavior
   - Institutions shape ethics
