Title: On the Measurement of Inequality
Authors: Anthony B. Atkinson
Year: 1970
Journal: Journal of Economic Theory, Vol. 2, No. 3, pp. 244-263
DOI: 10.1016/0022-0531(70)90039-6

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This foundational paper introduces what is now known as the Atkinson Inequality
Index, a normative measure with high sensitivity to societal inequality. The
paper develops a framework for measuring inequality that explicitly incorporates
social welfare judgments.

The Atkinson index produces an I value ranging between 0 and 1. An I value
closer to 0 indicates greater equality, while an I value closer to 1 signifies
higher inequality. The key innovation is the introduction of an inequality
aversion parameter (ε) that allows the measure to reflect different social
preferences regarding inequality.

In the Atkinson framework, social welfare is the product of mean income and
equality so that even if rises in inequality pull down the second factor, the
effect may be offset by increases in the first. We must judge the outcome by
both factors, not just one.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. ATKINSON INDEX: Introduces inequality measure with explicit welfare foundation

2. INEQUALITY AVERSION (ε): Parameter captures society's preferences about
   redistribution - higher ε means more aversion to inequality

3. WELFARE INTERPRETATION: Index measures the proportion of income that could
   be discarded while maintaining same welfare if distributed equally

4. NORMATIVE FOUNDATION: Unlike Gini, explicitly incorporates value judgments
   about inequality

5. TRANSFER SENSITIVITY: Index satisfies Pigou-Dalton transfer principle -
   transfers from rich to poor reduce measured inequality

6. POLICY RELEVANCE: Framework connects inequality measurement to welfare
   economics and policy evaluation
