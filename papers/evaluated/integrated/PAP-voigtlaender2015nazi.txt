Title: Nazi Indoctrination and Anti-Semitic Beliefs in Germany
Authors: Nico Voigtländer, Hans-Joachim Voth
Year: 2015
Journal: Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, Vol. 112, No. 26, pp. 7931-7936
DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1414822112

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

LANDMARK PAPER on belief modification through indoctrination.

KEY FINDING:
Nazi indoctrination was HIGHLY EFFECTIVE.

COHORT ANALYSIS:
Germans who grew up under the Nazi regime are MUCH MORE anti-Semitic
today than those born before or after that period.

EFFECT SIZE:
2-3x higher committed anti-Semitism rate among Nazi-schooled cohort.

PERSISTENCE:
Effect persists 50+ years (surveys from 1996 and 2006).

WHAT WAS MOST EFFECTIVE?
- SCHOOLING was most effective
- NOT radio or cinema propaganda

AMPLIFICATION MECHANISM:
Where schooling could tap into PRE-EXISTING PREJUDICES, indoctrination
was particularly strong. → CONFIRMATION BIAS

POLICY IMPLICATION:
Beliefs CAN be modified massively through policy intervention.

CRITICAL PERIOD:
Childhood/youth as window for belief formation.

COMPANION TO 2012 QJE PAPER:
- 2012 = STOCK (historical accumulation)
- 2015 (this paper) = FLOW (indoctrination adding to stock)

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Nazi indoctrination highly effective (2-3x effect)
2. Effect persists 50+ years
3. Schooling MORE effective than media propaganda
4. Pre-existing prejudice AMPLIFIES effect (confirmation bias)
5. Beliefs CAN be massively modified through policy
6. FLOW model: indoctrination adds to historical stock
