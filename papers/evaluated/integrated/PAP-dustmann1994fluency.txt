Title: Speaking Fluency, Writing Fluency and Earnings of Migrants
Authors: Christian Dustmann
Year: 1994
Journal: Journal of Population Economics, Vol. 7, No. 2, pp. 133-156
DOI: 10.1007/BF00173616

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper analyzes the determinants of language abilities of migrant workers
and the impact of language proficiency on their earnings position. The analysis
is based on data for West Germany.

PART 1: Ordered probit analysis of determinants of German speaking and writing
fluency for both male and female migrants. The data allow analysis not only of
migrants' personal characteristics but additionally of:
- Effect of family context on language fluency
- Effect of illiteracy on language fluency

PART 2: Effect of language on migrant's earnings position.

KEY FINDING: Language abilities, and ESPECIALLY WRITING PROFICIENCY,
considerably improve the earnings position of migrants.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Both speaking and writing fluency affect earnings
2. WRITING fluency has larger effect than speaking fluency
3. Family context matters for language acquisition
4. Illiteracy in origin country affects language learning
5. Gender differences in determinants of fluency
6. Data: West Germany migrant workers

================================================================================
METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTION
================================================================================

- Ordered probit for language proficiency determinants
- Separates speaking vs writing fluency (not commonly done)
- Family context variables included
- Accounts for illiteracy effects
