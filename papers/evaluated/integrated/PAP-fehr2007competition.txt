ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This chapter surveys the experimental evidence on social preferences and
new theoretical developments that model fairness, reciprocity, and altruism.
We review ultimatum games, public goods games, trust games, and gift exchange
experiments. We discuss models of inequity aversion, reciprocity, and
altruism, and how they can explain the observed behavioral patterns.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. EXPERIMENTAL EVIDENCE
   - Ultimatum games: fair offers, rejection of unfair offers
   - Public goods: conditional cooperation, punishment
   - Trust games: trust and trustworthiness
   - Gift exchange: effort responds to wages

2. THEORETICAL MODELS
   - Fehr-Schmidt inequity aversion
   - Bolton-Ockenfels ERC model
   - Rabin's reciprocity model
   - Charness-Rabin model

3. MODEL COMPARISON
   - Inequity aversion: outcome-based
   - Reciprocity: intention-based
   - Each captures different aspects

4. OPEN QUESTIONS
   - Role of intentions vs. outcomes
   - Individual heterogeneity
   - Context dependence
