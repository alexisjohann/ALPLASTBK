Title: Breakdown of Will
Authors: George Ainslie
Year: 2001

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Behavioral science has long been puzzled by the experience of temptation, the
resulting impulsiveness, and the variably successful control of this
impulsiveness. This book presents evidence that contradicts rational models in
which discounting the value of future events at a constant rate keeps
preference consistent.

Both people and nonhuman animals discount the value of expected events in a
hyperbolic curve (where value is divided approximately by expected delay),
which is more bowed than the rational, exponential curve. With hyperbolic
discounting, options that pay off quickly will be temporarily preferred to
richer but slower-paying alternatives.

The "breakdown" in the title refers to breaking the will down like one breaks
down a mechanical device into its constituent parts. The will is neither a
mysterious organ nor an idle cog - it is a bargaining situation in which
temporally distinct populations of partially cooperating interests compete for
selection. Self-control is a matter of reasonable self-prediction.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. PICOECONOMICS: Just as microeconomics describes negotiation among
   individuals, picoeconomics describes interactions among parts within the
   individual for control of behavioral capacity

2. HYPERBOLIC OVER EXPONENTIAL: Empirical evidence shows hyperbolic, not
   exponential, discount curves in both humans and animals

3. WILL AS BARGAINING: Will is not a faculty but a bargaining process among
   temporally distinct motivational states

4. SELF-PREDICTION: Self-control depends on accurate prediction of one's
   own future behavior and preferences

5. INTERTEMPORAL COOPERATION: Successive motivational states must cooperate
   like members of a repeated game

6. AKRASIA EXPLAINED: The ancient puzzle of weakness of will (akrasia) is
   resolved through hyperbolic discounting and intertemporal bargaining
