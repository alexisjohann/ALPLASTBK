Title: Does Fairness Prevent Market Clearing? An Experimental Investigation
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Erich Kirchler, Andreas Weichbold, Simon Gächter
Journal: Quarterly Journal of Economics
Volume: 108, Number 2, Pages 437-459
Year: 1993

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper experimentally investigates whether fairness concerns prevent
markets from clearing. We examine how fairness norms and reciprocity
influence market outcomes, particularly in labor markets where workers
may reject "unfair" wages even at the cost of unemployment.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. FAIRNESS AND MARKET CLEARING
   - Fairness concerns cause market inefficiencies
   - Workers reject unfair offers despite unemployment
   - Wages remain above market-clearing levels

2. RECIPROCITY IN MARKETS
   - Positive reciprocity rewards fair treatment
   - Negative reciprocity punishes unfair offers
   - Bilateral interactions shape outcomes

3. IMPLICATIONS FOR LABOR ECONOMICS
   - Explains involuntary unemployment
   - Fairness constraints on wage setting
   - Efficiency wage interpretation
