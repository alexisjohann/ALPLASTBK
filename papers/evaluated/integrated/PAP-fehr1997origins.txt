Title: Origins of Reciprocal Preferences
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: Evolution and Human Behavior
Year: 1997

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines the evolutionary origins of reciprocal preferences.
We argue that strong reciprocity - cooperation and punishment in one-shot
interactions - evolved through gene-culture coevolution and cultural group
selection. This explains human cooperation beyond what kin selection or
direct reciprocity can account for.
