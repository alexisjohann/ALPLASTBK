================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper CHALLENGES TWO FUNDAMENTAL TENETS of lifecycle investing:

TENET 1: Diversify across stocks and bonds
TENET 2: Reduce equity allocations with age

METHOD: Estimate optimal age-based weights in a lifecycle model that
nonparametrically preserves time-series and cross-sectional dependencies
in asset class returns.

KEY FINDINGS:

OPTIMAL ALLOCATION (age-invariant!):
- 1/3 DOMESTIC STOCKS (33%)
- 2/3 INTERNATIONAL STOCKS (67%)
- 0% BONDS (no material fixed income allocation)

This strategy DOMINATES conventional stock-bond strategies in:
- Building wealth
- Preserving capital
- Generating bequests

CRITICAL INSIGHT:
Investors prefer diversifying with INTERNATIONAL STOCKS, not bonds.

CONSEQUENCE FOR TARGET-DATE FUNDS:
TDF investors need 63% MORE preretirement savings to match the optimal
strategy's expected retirement utility.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. TENET 1 WRONG: Stock-bond diversification suboptimal
2. TENET 2 WRONG: Age-based equity reduction suboptimal
3. Optimal: 1/3 domestic + 2/3 international stocks
4. Optimal bonds allocation: 0%
5. Allocation is AGE-INVARIANT (constant across lifecycle)
6. TDF investors need 63% more savings for same utility
