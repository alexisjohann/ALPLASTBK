Title: Efficiency and Fairness in Collective Decision-Making
Authors: Ernst Fehr
Journal: Journal of Economic Literature
Year: 1998

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines the relationship between efficiency and fairness in
collective decision-making contexts. We analyze how fairness concerns
affect group decisions and the resulting efficiency implications.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. EFFICIENCY-FAIRNESS TRADE-OFF
   - Fairness concerns can reduce efficiency
   - Groups balance both objectives
   - Context determines priorities

2. COLLECTIVE DECISION DYNAMICS
   - Group composition matters
   - Voting mechanisms affect outcomes
   - Communication improves coordination

3. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Design for both efficiency and fairness
   - Institutional rules matter
   - Transparency supports better outcomes
