Title: The Long-Run Performance of Natural Experiments: Large Sample Evidence
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter, Georg Kirchsteiger
Journal: Review of Economics and Statistics
Year: 2004

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines the long-run performance implications of natural experiments.
Using large sample evidence, we analyze how behavioral factors and reciprocity
affect productivity and economic outcomes over extended time periods.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. LONG-RUN EFFECTS
   - Natural experiments show persistent effects
   - Behavioral patterns stabilize over time
   - Selection and treatment effects distinguished

2. PRODUCTIVITY DYNAMICS
   - Reciprocity affects long-run productivity
   - Trust builds over time
   - Institutional context matters

3. METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTIONS
   - Large sample validation
   - Natural experiment identification
   - Long-run estimation strategies
