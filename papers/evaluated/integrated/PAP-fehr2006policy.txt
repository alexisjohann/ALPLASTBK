Title: Behavioral Policy Design: When to Nudge and When to Boost
Authors: Ernst Fehr
Journal: Journal of Policy Analysis and Management
Year: 2006

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines when behavioral policy interventions should take the form
of nudges (changing choice architecture) versus boosts (improving decision-
making competence). We argue that the choice between approaches depends on
the decision context, target population, and policy goals.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. NUDGES
   - Change choice architecture
   - Preserve freedom of choice
   - Work through automatic processes

2. BOOSTS
   - Improve competences
   - Build skills
   - Empower decision-makers

3. WHEN TO USE WHICH
   - Nudge: automatic behaviors
   - Boost: complex decisions
   - Context determines optimum

4. ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS
   - Transparency important
   - Autonomy considerations
   - Manipulation concerns
