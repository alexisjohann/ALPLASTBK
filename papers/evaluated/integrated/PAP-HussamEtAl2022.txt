Title: Rational Habit Formation: Experimental Evidence from Handwashing in India
Authors: Reshmaan Hussam, Atonu Rabbani, Giovanni Reggiani, Natalia Rigol
Year: 2022
Journal: American Economic Journal: Applied Economics, Vol. 14, No. 1, pp. 1-41

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The researchers test the predictions of the rational addiction model,
reconceptualized as rational habit formation, in the context of handwashing
in rural India. To track handwashing, they design soap dispensers with timed
sensors.

They test for rational habit formation by informing some households about a
future change in the returns to daily handwashing. Monitoring and incentives
raise handwashing contemporaneously, and effects persist well after they end.

In addition, people are rational about this habit formation: when they
anticipate future monitoring, they increase their current handwashing.
Average child weight and height increase for all study arms given soap
dispensers. Frontloading both financial and social incentives facilitates
habituation, and agents internalize this habitual nature.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. RATIONAL ANTICIPATION: People increase current behavior when they know
   future monitoring or incentives are coming

2. HABIT PERSISTENCE: Effects of monitoring and incentives persist well
   after intervention ends

3. SENSOR TRACKING: Innovative soap dispensers with timed sensors enable
   precise measurement of handwashing

4. FRONTLOADING WORKS: Frontloading incentives (financial and social)
   facilitates faster habit formation

5. HEALTH OUTCOMES: Average child weight and height increase with intervention

6. INTERNALIZATION: Agents internalize the habitual nature of the behavior
   and respond rationally to it
