Title: The Long-term Effects of Price Incentives on Trust-Building
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Bettina Rockenbach
Journal: Experimental Economics
Year: 2005

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines how price incentives affect trust-building in the long
term. We show that while incentives can increase short-term compliance, they
may undermine trust-building and have negative long-term effects. The
signaling properties of incentives matter for relationship development.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. SHORT-TERM EFFECTS
   - Incentives increase compliance
   - Immediate behavioral response
   - Clear effect on outcomes

2. LONG-TERM EFFECTS
   - Trust development affected
   - Crowding out possible
   - Relationship quality suffers

3. MECHANISM
   - Incentives signal distrust
   - Self-perception changes
   - Intrinsic motivation reduced
