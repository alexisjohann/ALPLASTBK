Title: Does Fairness Prevent Market Clearing?
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter, Georg Kirchsteiger
Journal: Quarterly Journal of Economics
Year: 1993

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines whether fairness considerations prevent competitive markets
from clearing. Using experimental labor markets, we show that workers who
receive wages above market-clearing levels reciprocate with higher effort.
This creates an efficiency wage equilibrium where wages remain above
competitive levels and unemployment persists.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. FAIRNESS AND MARKET CLEARING
   - Wages systematically above market-clearing levels
   - Workers reciprocate fair wages with effort
   - Unemployment emerges in equilibrium

2. GIFT EXCHANGE IN LABOR MARKETS
   - Fair wage offers elicit higher effort
   - Unfair wages lead to minimal effort
   - Reciprocity creates efficiency wages

3. EXPERIMENTAL EVIDENCE
   - Labor market experiments
   - Gift exchange observed
   - Persistent non-clearing
