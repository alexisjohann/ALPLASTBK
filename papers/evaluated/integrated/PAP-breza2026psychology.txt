ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Research agenda for BEHAVIORAL DEVELOPMENT ECONOMICS.

CENTRAL THESIS:
POVERTY AMPLIFIES psychological biases.

PSYCHOLOGICAL CHANNELS IN DEVELOPMENT:
(i) Impeding individuals' ability to climb out of poverty
(ii) Shaping organization of production
(iii) Functioning of markets
(iv) Role of informal institutions

FIVE FEATURES OF POVERTY THAT AMPLIFY CONSTRAINTS:

1. PROXIMITY TO SUBSISTENCE → Higher stakes
2. HIGH VOLATILITY → Stress, reduced bandwidth
3. MARKET FAILURES → No market correction of biases
4. WEAK FORMAL INSTITUTIONS → No safety nets
5. RELIANCE ON SOCIAL TIES → Social norms dominant

MISSING MARKETS LENS:
- Tools/products/services against biases are UNDERSUPPLIED
- Behavioral biases have LARGE STEADY-STATE IMPACTS
- Even among highly experienced agents

FIVE PSYCHOLOGICAL CONSTRUCTS REVIEWED:
1. Self-control
2. Cognitive constraints
3. Self-beliefs
4. Mental health
5. Social norms

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Poverty AMPLIFIES psychological biases
2. Five poverty features amplify constraints
3. Missing markets lens: bias tools undersupplied
4. Biases have large steady-state impacts
5. Five psychological constructs: self-control, cognition, beliefs, mental health, norms
6. Explicitly argues context amplifies biases (Ψ-framework)
