Title: Language Training and Refugees' Integration
Authors: Jacob Nielsen Arendt, Iben Bolvig, Mette Foged, Linea Hasager, Giovanni Peri
Year: 2022
Journal: Review of Economics and Statistics
NBER Working Paper: No. 26834 (2020)

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The authors evaluate a Danish reform focused on improving Danish language
training for those granted refugee status on or after January 1, 1999.

IDENTIFICATION: Using a Regression Discontinuity Design, they find a
SIGNIFICANT, PERMANENT, POSITIVE effect on earnings.

The new program was MANDATORY for refugees and reunified family members.
Individuals who obtained refugee status in Denmark on or after January 1, 1999
were entitled to (mandated to) attend the expanded language program. Those who
obtained refugee status before that date did not qualify and only had access
to more limited language training.

KEY FINDING: Language training combined with placement in STRONG LABOR MARKETS
significantly improved refugees' LONG-RUN labor market outcomes.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. RDD using 1999 reform cutoff date
2. Permanent positive effect on earnings
3. Language training effects larger in strong labor markets
4. Mandatory program for post-1999 refugees
5. Long-run integration benefits documented
6. Denmark 1999 reform as natural experiment
