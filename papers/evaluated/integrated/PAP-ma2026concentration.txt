================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

NEW DATA documenting long-run evolution of firm size distribution in
TEN MARKET-BASED ECONOMIES (Asia, Europe, North America, Oceania)
over 120 YEARS (1900-2020).

COVERAGE: Comprehensive coverage of population of firms.

KEY FINDINGS:

1. SALES CONCENTRATION: Increased around the world
2. NET INCOME CONCENTRATION: Increased around the world
3. EQUITY CAPITAL CONCENTRATION: Increased around the world
4. EMPLOYMENT CONCENTRATION: STABLE over the long run (most cases)

THESE TRENDS HOLD:
- In the aggregate
- At the industry level

CRITICAL INSIGHT:
Large firms often achieve GREATER SCALE without proportionally
more workers.

IMPLICATIONS:
- Rising dominance of large firms is a PERVASIVE phenomenon
- NOT limited to recent decades
- NOT limited to the United States

Fundamental evidence for ANTITRUST POLICY and MARKET POWER debates.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Sales/income/equity concentration increased globally (1900-2020)
2. Employment concentration remained STABLE
3. Trends hold across industries and countries
4. Large firms scale without proportional workforce
5. Not just recent US phenomenon - global and historical
6. 10 economies, 120 years of data
