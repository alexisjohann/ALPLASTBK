Title: The Economics of Temporary Migrations
Authors: Christian Dustmann, Joseph-Simon Görlach
Year: 2016
Journal: Journal of Economic Literature, Vol. 54, No. 1, pp. 98-136
DOI: 10.1257/jel.54.1.98

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Many migrations are temporary — a fact that has often been ignored in the
economic literature on migration. Such omission may be serious in that expected
migration temporariness can impart a distinct dynamic element to immigrants'
economic behavior, generating possible consequences for nonmigrants in both
home and host countries.

The paper provides a thorough examination of the various aspects of temporary
migrations that matter for the analysis of economic phenomena.

KEY CONTRIBUTIONS:
1. Demonstrates the extent of temporary migrations in population movements
2. Shows how temporariness affects various economic choices
3. Documents how better data improved measurement and analysis
4. Proposes a general theoretical framework for modeling temporary migration
5. Outlines various motives for temporariness
6. Discusses consequences for nonmigrants in home and host countries

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Temporary migration is MORE COMMON than permanent migration
2. Expected temporariness affects labor supply, savings, human capital investment
3. Return intentions shape remittance behavior
4. Better longitudinal data crucial for understanding migration dynamics
5. Three main motives: price differences, consumption complementarities, human capital
6. Consequences for both sending and receiving countries

================================================================================
THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

The paper proposes a unified framework with three return motives:
- (i) Differences in relative prices between host and home country
- (ii) Complementarities between consumption and location
- (iii) Human capital accumulation abroad enhancing home-country earnings
