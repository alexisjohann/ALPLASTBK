Title: Cooperative Behavior Declines in Succeeding Periods of Public Goods Games
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter, Georg Kirchsteiger
Journal: Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization
Year: 1994

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper documents the decline of cooperation in repeated public goods games.
We show that cooperation levels systematically decrease over time in finite
horizon games, but the pattern depends on group composition and institutional
design. Understanding this decay is crucial for designing institutions that
sustain cooperation.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. COOPERATION DECAY
   - Cooperation declines over periods
   - End-game effects observed
   - Not purely strategic unraveling

2. CONDITIONAL COOPERATION
   - Many subjects are conditional cooperators
   - Decline driven by free-riders
   - Heterogeneity in types matters

3. INSTITUTIONAL IMPLICATIONS
   - Design can slow decay
   - Communication helps
   - Punishment opportunities stabilize
