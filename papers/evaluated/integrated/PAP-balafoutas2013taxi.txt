What Drives Taxi Drivers? A Field Experiment on Fraud in a Market for Credence Goods
================================================================================

Authors: Balafoutas, Loukas and Beck, Adrian and Kerschbamer, Rudolf and Sutter, Matthias
Year: 2013
Journal: The Review of Economic Studies, 80(3), 876-891
DOI: 10.1093/restud/rds049

Source: Abstract fetched via WebSearch (2026-01-30)

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper presents results of a controlled field experiment on the extent and
determinants of fraudulent behavior in the provision of taxi rides as a credence
good. The contribution includes exact and separate measurement of two problems:
overtreatment (taking longer routes) and overcharging (charging higher prices).

Using portable GPS loggers, we tracked taxi drivers' routes in Athens, Greece,
meter by meter, to measure costly overtreatment.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

- Taxi drivers exploit informational advantage over NON-LOCAL passengers:
  * Non-Athenian Greeks and foreigners taken on detours ~2x longer than locals
  * Local passengers were hardly ever taken on detours

- Overcharging patterns:
  * ~20% of foreigners were overcharged
  * ~5% of Greek non-locals were overcharged
  * Foreigners exploited regarding tariff system knowledge

- Income signaling effect:
  * High-income passengers taken on longer routes
  * High-income passengers overcharged slightly more than low-income

- Two distinct fraud types measured separately:
  * Overtreatment: unnecessary service (longer routes)
  * Overcharging: higher prices for same service

================================================================================
METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

- Study Design: Controlled Field Experiment
- Sample: Taxi rides in Athens, Greece
- Identification: GPS tracking (meter-by-meter route measurement)
- Treatments: Passenger type (local/non-local Greek/foreigner), income signal
- Key innovation: Separate measurement of overtreatment vs. overcharging
