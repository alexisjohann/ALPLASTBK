================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

SCARCITY steals MENTAL CAPACITY wherever it occurs:
- The hungry
- The lonely
- The time-strapped
- The poor

KEY MECHANISM:
If the mind is focused on one thing, other abilities and skills suffer:
- ATTENTION
- SELF-CONTROL
- LONG-TERM PLANNING

COMPUTER ANALOGY:
Like a computer running multiple programs, our mental processors begin
to slow down.

CORE CONCEPT - SCARCITY TRAP:
Our brains so tightly focus on what is desperately lacking that thinking
about ANYTHING ELSE becomes tremendously difficult.

BANDWIDTH:
The limited number of things any human can focus on at one time.

CONSEQUENCES:
- Limited bandwidth
- Tunneling (focus on scarcity)
- Depletes self-control
- Leads to IMPULSIVE BEHAVIOR

APPLICATIONS:
- Poverty: Why the poor make "bad" decisions
- Time pressure: Why busy people drop balls
- Loneliness: Why isolated people struggle socially
- Dieting: Why the hungry overeat

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Scarcity captures attention and reduces bandwidth
2. Tunneling: focus on lack crowds out other thinking
3. Depleted bandwidth → reduced self-control
4. Applies across domains: money, time, food, social
5. Explains "irrational" behavior of the poor
6. Foundational for behavioral poverty research
