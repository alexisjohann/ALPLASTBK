ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper applies behavioral economics insights to environmental protection.
We examine how psychological factors - loss aversion, social norms, defaults,
and framing - can be leveraged to promote pro-environmental behavior.
Traditional policy tools can be enhanced with behavioral approaches.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. BEHAVIORAL BARRIERS
   - Present bias delays action
   - Loss aversion for status quo
   - Information overload

2. SOCIAL NORMS
   - Peer effects matter
   - Descriptive norms effective
   - Injunctive norms less so

3. CHOICE ARCHITECTURE
   - Green defaults
   - Framing effects
   - Simplification helps

4. POLICY DESIGN
   - Combine with traditional tools
   - Test and iterate
   - Context matters
