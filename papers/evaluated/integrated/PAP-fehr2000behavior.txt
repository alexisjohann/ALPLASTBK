ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper discusses the economics of reciprocity - the behavioral response to
perceived kindness and unkindness. We survey experimental evidence showing
that reciprocity is a powerful determinant of human behavior, and discuss
the economic implications of reciprocal preferences for contract enforcement,
public goods provision, and competitive markets.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. POSITIVE RECIPROCITY
   - People reward kind actions
   - Gift exchange in labor markets
   - Efficiency wages as reciprocal response

2. NEGATIVE RECIPROCITY
   - People punish unkind actions
   - Rejection in ultimatum games
   - Costly punishment in public goods

3. DETERMINANTS
   - Intentions matter, not just outcomes
   - Reference points affect perceptions
   - Context frames what counts as kind/unkind

4. ECONOMIC IMPLICATIONS
   - Contract enforcement without courts
   - Wage rigidity and unemployment
   - Tax compliance and public goods
