Title: Third-Party Punishment and Social Norms
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Urs Fischbacher
Journal: Evolution and Human Behavior
Volume: 25, Number 2, Pages 63-87
Year: 2004

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines third-party punishment - punishment by individuals who
are not directly affected by norm violations. Using experimental games, we
show that uninvolved third parties willingly punish norm violators at
personal cost. Third-party punishment is essential for understanding how
social norms are enforced in large groups.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. THIRD-PARTY PUNISHMENT
   - Uninvolved observers punish
   - Costly to the punisher
   - Not explained by self-interest

2. NORM ENFORCEMENT
   - Enables large-scale norms
   - Decentralized monitoring
   - Multiplier effect on deterrence

3. EXPERIMENTAL EVIDENCE
   - Dictator game with punishment
   - Third parties punish unfair allocations
   - Punishment proportional to violation

4. EVOLUTIONARY IMPLICATIONS
   - Extends beyond direct reciprocity
   - Cultural group selection
   - Uniquely human capacity
