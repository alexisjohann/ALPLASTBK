Title: Golden Eggs and Hyperbolic Discounting
Authors: David Laibson
Year: 1997
Journal: Quarterly Journal of Economics, Vol. 112, No. 2, pp. 443-478

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Hyperbolic discount functions induce dynamically inconsistent preferences,
implying a motive for consumers to constrain their own future choices. This
paper analyzes the decisions of a hyperbolic consumer who has access to an
imperfect commitment technology: an illiquid asset whose sale must be initiated
one period before the sale proceeds are received.

The paper introduces the "quasi-hyperbolic" or "beta-delta" discount function,
which approximates hyperbolic discounting in discrete time with two parameters:
β (present bias) and δ (standard exponential discount factor). This retains
analytical tractability while capturing the key qualitative feature of
hyperbolic discounting: excessive impatience for immediate rewards.

The model predicts that consumption tracks income, and explains why consumers
have asset-specific marginal propensities to consume. Financial innovation may
have caused declining U.S. savings rates by increasing liquidity and eliminating
commitment opportunities. Financial market innovation may reduce welfare by
providing "too much" liquidity.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. QUASI-HYPERBOLIC MODEL: β-δ discounting captures present bias parsimoniously

2. DYNAMIC INCONSISTENCY: Hyperbolic discounters want to constrain future
   selves

3. COMMITMENT DEMAND: Illiquid assets serve as commitment devices (golden eggs)

4. CONSUMPTION TRACKING: Model explains consumption-income correlation

5. ASSET-SPECIFIC MPC: Different marginal propensity to consume from different
   assets

6. LIQUIDITY PARADOX: Financial innovation can REDUCE welfare by eliminating
   commitment
