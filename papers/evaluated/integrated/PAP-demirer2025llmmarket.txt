Title: The Emerging Market for Intelligence: Pricing, Supply, and Demand for LLMs
Authors: Mert Demirer, Andrey Fradkin, Nadav Tadelis, Sida Peng
Year: 2025
Type: NBER Working Paper No. 34608
DOI: 10.3386/w34608

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

SIX FACTS about the structure and dynamics of the LLM market using API
usage data from OpenRouter and Microsoft Azure.

FACT 1: RAPID GROWTH
- Number of models, creators, and inference providers growing
- Driven by OPEN-SOURCE entrants

FACT 2: PRICE DECLINES AND HETEROGENEITY
- Persistent price heterogeneity across and within intelligence tiers
- Open-source models are 90% CHEAPER than comparable closed-source
  models of same intelligence

FACT 3: MARKET DYNAMISM
- Frequent turnover among leading models and creators

FACT 4: HORIZONTAL AND VERTICAL DIFFERENTIATION
- No single model dominates across use cases
- Demand for intelligence varies widely across applications

FACT 5: LIMITED JEVONS PARADOX
- Price elasticities just above one (~1.0-1.1)
- Limited scope for Jevons-Paradox effects (efficiency gains don't
  dramatically increase consumption)

FACT 6: MULTI-MODEL EXPERIMENTATION
- Share of firms using multiple models increased over time
- BUT most firms concentrate on single model
- Pattern consistent with experimentation rather than persistent
  multi-model reliance

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Rapid LLM market growth driven by open-source
2. Open-source 90% cheaper at same intelligence
3. High turnover among leading models
4. No single model dominates (differentiation)
5. Price elasticity ~1 (limited Jevons effects)
6. Multi-model use = experimentation, not persistent diversification
