ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This handbook chapter examines the relationship between intrinsic motivation
and extrinsic incentives. We review evidence that external rewards can
crowd out intrinsic motivation, leading to lower performance or quality.
Understanding this interaction is crucial for designing effective incentive
systems.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. CROWDING OUT EFFECT
   - Extrinsic rewards reduce intrinsic motivation
   - Hidden costs of rewards
   - Context-dependent effect

2. MECHANISMS
   - Self-determination undermined
   - Signals distrust
   - Changes self-perception

3. WHEN CROWDING OCCURS
   - Tasks with intrinsic interest
   - Controlling incentives
   - Signals about ability

4. DESIGN IMPLICATIONS
   - Use incentives carefully
   - Consider crowding effects
   - Support autonomy
