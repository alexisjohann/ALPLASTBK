Title: Advances in Prospect Theory: Cumulative Representation of Uncertainty
Authors: Amos Tversky, Daniel Kahneman
Year: 1992
Journal: Journal of Risk and Uncertainty, Vol. 5, No. 4, pp. 297-323
DOI: 10.1007/BF00122574

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper presents CUMULATIVE PROSPECT THEORY (CPT), an extension of
Prospect Theory (1979) that applies to uncertain as well as risky prospects
with any number of outcomes.

KEY INNOVATIONS:
1. CUMULATIVE PROBABILITY WEIGHTING: Weights are applied to cumulative
   rather than individual probabilities, avoiding violations of stochastic
   dominance that could occur in the original formulation.

2. RANK-DEPENDENT WEIGHTING: Outcomes are ranked, and probability weights
   depend on the rank position—more extreme outcomes receive more weight.

3. SEPARATE FUNCTIONS FOR GAINS AND LOSSES: Different probability weighting
   functions w⁺(p) for gains and w⁻(p) for losses.

CANONICAL PARAMETERS (Median estimates from experimental data):
- Loss aversion: λ = 2.25
- Value function curvature (gains): α = 0.88
- Value function curvature (losses): β = 0.88
- Probability weighting (gains): γ = 0.61
- Probability weighting (losses): δ = 0.69

VALUE FUNCTION: v(x) = x^α for gains, v(x) = -λ(-x)^β for losses

PROBABILITY WEIGHTING: w(p) = p^γ / [p^γ + (1-p)^γ]^(1/γ)
This function overweights small probabilities and underweights large ones.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Cumulative weighting resolves stochastic dominance violations
2. Canonical parameters: λ=2.25, α=β=0.88, γ=0.61, δ=0.69
3. Probability weighting differs for gains vs. losses
4. Model applies to continuous distributions, not just discrete
5. Rank-dependence explains observed patterns in lottery choice
6. Foundation for modern behavioral economics parameter estimation
