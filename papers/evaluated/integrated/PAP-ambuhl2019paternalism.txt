Title: Evaluating Deliberative Competence: A Simple Method with an Application to Financial Choice
Authors: Sandro Ambühl, B. Douglas Bernheim, Annamaria Lusardi
Year: 2022
Journal: American Economic Review, Vol. 112, No. 11, pp. 3584-3626
Note: Originally NBER Working Paper No. 20618 (2014), revised 2017

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The paper develops methods for evaluating opportunity-neutral interventions
designed to improve the quality of decision making in settings where people
imperfectly comprehend consequences. The authors introduce a framework for
assessing "deliberative competence" - the ability to make decisions that align
with one's own underlying preferences when fully informed.

The application focuses on financial education interventions and their
effectiveness in improving financial decision-making quality. The methodology
allows researchers to evaluate whether interventions genuinely improve
decision quality versus merely changing behavior.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. DELIBERATIVE COMPETENCE: Introduces concept of decision-making quality
   independent of specific outcomes

2. NEUTRAL EVALUATION: Method evaluates interventions without imposing
   external welfare criteria

3. FINANCIAL LITERACY: Application shows financial education can improve
   decision quality in some domains

4. COMPREHENSION GAPS: Identifies specific areas where people fail to
   understand financial consequences

5. WELFARE NEUTRAL: Framework respects heterogeneous preferences while
   measuring comprehension

6. POLICY DESIGN: Provides tools for designing and evaluating educational
   interventions
