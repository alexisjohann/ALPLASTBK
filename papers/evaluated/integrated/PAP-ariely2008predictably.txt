ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This bestselling book by behavioral economist Dan Ariely demonstrates through
clever experiments that human irrationality is not random but SYSTEMATIC and
PREDICTABLE—hence "predictably irrational."

KEY PHENOMENA DOCUMENTED:

1. RELATIVITY: We evaluate options comparatively, not absolutely.
   - Decoy effects: A clearly inferior option makes another look better
   - The Economist subscription experiment

2. ARBITRARY COHERENCE: Initial anchors become reference points.
   - Social Security number → auction bids correlation
   - First prices become anchors for future valuations

3. THE COST OF ZERO: "Free" is special, not just a low price.
   - Amazon free shipping effect
   - Lindt truffle vs. Hershey's Kiss experiment

4. SOCIAL VS. MARKET NORMS: These operate by different rules.
   - Asking for help vs. paying for help
   - AARP lawyers example

5. THE EFFECT OF EXPECTATIONS: What we expect shapes what we get.
   - Vinegar in beer experiment
   - Placebo effects in pain medication

6. THE PROBLEM OF PROCRASTINATION:
   - Pre-commitment devices
   - Self-control as limited resource

7. OWNERSHIP AND THE ENDOWMENT EFFECT:
   - Duke basketball tickets experiment
   - Virtual ownership creates attachment

8. HONESTY AND DISHONESTY:
   - People cheat a little but not a lot
   - Moral reminders reduce cheating

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Irrationality is SYSTEMATIC, not random
2. Relativity and decoy effects shape choices
3. "Free" has disproportionate psychological power
4. Social and market norms don't mix well
5. Expectations create self-fulfilling prophecies
6. Pre-commitment can overcome procrastination
7. 19+ citations in EBF framework
