Title: Do Workers Work More if Wages Are High? Evidence from a Randomized Experiment
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Simon Gächter
Journal: American Economic Review
Year: 2001

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper uses a randomized experiment to test whether workers work more
when wages are high. We find strong evidence for gift exchange: workers
receiving unexpectedly high wages provide significantly more effort.
This supports efficiency wage theory and demonstrates the importance of
fairness in labor markets.
