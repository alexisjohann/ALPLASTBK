Title: The Economics of Modern Manufacturing: Technology, Strategy, and Organization
Authors: Paul R. Milgrom, John Roberts
Year: 1990
Journal: American Economic Review, Vol. 80, No. 3, pp. 511-528

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Manufacturing is undergoing a revolution. The mass production model is being
replaced by a vision of a flexible multiproduct firm that emphasizes quality
and speedy response to market conditions while utilizing technologically
advanced equipment and new forms of organization.

The authors' optimizing model of the firm generates many of the observed patterns
that mark modern manufacturing. Central to the results is a method of handling
optimization and comparative statics problems that requires neither
differentiability nor convexity.

This paper introduced the concept of SUPERMODULARITY to economics, which became
central to analyzing complementarities in organizational design. Industries are
transformed by the adoption of flexible production technologies and
complementary changes in firms' organization.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. COMPLEMENTARITIES: Activities that reinforce each other should be adopted
   together

2. SUPERMODULARITY: Mathematical framework for analyzing complementarities

3. FLEXIBLE MANUFACTURING: Technology, organization, and strategy are
   complements

4. SYSTEM EFFECTS: Changing one practice increases returns to changing others

5. BUNDLE ADOPTION: Optimal to adopt entire bundle of complementary practices

6. ORGANIZATIONAL DESIGN: Production technology shapes optimal organization
