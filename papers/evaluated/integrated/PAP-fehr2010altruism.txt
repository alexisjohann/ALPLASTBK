Title: Altruism 3.0: Addressing the Gendered Dimensions of Altruism
Authors: Ernst Fehr, Urs Fischbacher
Journal: Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization
Year: 2010

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper examines gender differences in altruistic behavior. We analyze
how men and women differ in their prosocial preferences and how context
affects these gender gaps in altruism.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. GENDER DIFFERENCES
   - Women often more altruistic
   - Context moderates differences
   - Task-specific patterns

2. BEHAVIORAL MECHANISMS
   - Social image concerns
   - Risk preferences
   - Empathy differences

3. IMPLICATIONS
   - Policy targeting
   - Experimental design
   - Workplace dynamics
