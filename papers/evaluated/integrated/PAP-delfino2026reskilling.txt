Title: Unwilling to Reskill? Experimental Evidence from Real-World Jobseekers
Authors: Alexia Delfino, Andrea Garnero, Sergio Inferrera, Marco Leonardi, Raffaella Sadun
Year: 2026
Type: NBER Working Paper No. 34633
DOI: 10.3386/w34633

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

Study of BARRIERS preventing jobseekers from pursuing reskilling in
high-demand occupations.

METHOD: Discrete choice experiment among Italian jobseekers
TARGET OCCUPATIONS: IT assistant, Construction technician

KEY FINDINGS:

1. WILLINGNESS TO PAY DIFFERS BY OCCUPATION
   - WTP to reskill into IT: POSITIVE
   - WTP to reskill into construction: NEGATIVE (requires compensation)

2. WHAT EXPLAINS THE DIFFERENCE?
   - Beliefs about monetary returns: Some effect
   - Social status perceptions: Some effect
   - IDENTITY FIT: MOST IMPORTANT FACTOR

3. INFORMATION INTERVENTION WORKS
   Light-touch RCT providing data on occupational returns:
   - Increased stated interest in reskilling
   - Increased ACTUAL engagement in real-world training

THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTION:
Extends Akerlof-Kranton IDENTITY ECONOMICS (MS-IB-001) to labor market
reskilling decisions.

POLICY RELEVANCE:
For active labor market programs targeting occupational transitions.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Identity fit is MOST IMPORTANT factor for reskilling
2. WTP positive for IT, negative for construction
3. Light-touch information intervention increases real training
4. Monetary beliefs and status matter but less than identity
5. Extends Identity Economics to labor markets
6. Policy-relevant for active labor market programs
