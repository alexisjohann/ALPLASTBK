ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This paper applies behavioral economics to health decision-making. We examine
how cognitive biases, present bias, and social influences affect health
behaviors. Understanding these factors is crucial for designing effective
health policies and interventions.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. COGNITIVE BIASES
   - Optimism bias in health
   - Availability heuristic
   - Framing effects

2. PRESENT BIAS
   - Immediate gratification valued
   - Future health discounted
   - Explains unhealthy behaviors

3. SOCIAL INFLUENCES
   - Peer effects on health
   - Social norms matter
   - Network effects

4. POLICY IMPLICATIONS
   - Defaults for health choices
   - Commitment devices
   - Social norm interventions
