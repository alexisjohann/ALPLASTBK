Title: Immigrant Labor Market Integration across Admission Classes
Authors: Bernt Bratsberg, Oddbjørn Raaum, Knut Røed
Year: 2017
Journal: Nordic Economic Policy Review, Vol. 7, pp. 17-54
IZA Discussion Paper: No. 10513

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

The paper examines patterns of labor market integration across immigrant groups.

Using Norwegian longitudinal administrative data covering labor earnings and
social insurance claims over a 25-YEAR period, the authors present a
comprehensive picture of immigrant-native employment and social insurance
differentials by ADMISSION CLASS and by years since entry.

KEY FINDING: For refugees and family immigrants from low-income source
countries, the authors uncover ENCOURAGING SIGNS of labor market integration
during an initial period upon admission.

CRITICAL FINDING: After just 5-10 YEARS, the integration process GOES INTO
REVERSE with:
- Widening immigrant-native employment differentials
- Rising rates of immigrant social insurance dependency

Yet, the analysis reveals substantial HETEROGENEITY within admission class
and points to an important role of host-country schooling for successful
immigrant labor market integration.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. Initial integration encouraging (first 5 years)
2. Integration REVERSES after 5-10 years
3. Employment differentials widen over time
4. Social insurance dependency rises for immigrants
5. Substantial heterogeneity within admission class
6. Host-country education crucial for success
7. 25-year Norwegian administrative data
