Title: Rising International Migration of the Highly Skilled Transforms Demographic and Geographic Patterns of Flows in High-Income Countries: The Case of Switzerland 1966-2019
Authors: Mathias Lerch
Year: 2025
Journal: International Migration
DOI: 10.1111/imig.70057

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This study examines the DEMOGRAPHIC TRANSFORMATION of immigration to
Switzerland over 53 years (1966-2019), focusing on the rise of high-skilled
migrants.

KEY FINDINGS:

1. TERTIARY EDUCATION SHARE:
   - 1966-1970: Only 18% of immigrants had tertiary education
   - 2010-2018: Rose to 56% tertiary educated

2. GEOGRAPHIC SHIFT:
   - Anglo-Saxon and Western European immigrants: 87% tertiary educated
   - Traditional source countries (Southern Europe): declining share

3. BRAIN GAIN CONFIRMED:
   - Switzerland attracts POSITIVE SELECTION of migrants
   - Validates Borjas prediction: high-wage egalitarian destinations
     attract the most skilled workers from origin countries

METHODOLOGY: Longitudinal analysis using Swiss population registers,
1966-2019 (53 years of data).

POLICY RELEVANCE: Documents how bilateral agreements with EU and
selective immigration policies transformed Switzerland's immigrant
composition toward high-skilled workers.
