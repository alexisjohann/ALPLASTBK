Title: Social Neuroeconomics: The Neural Circuitry of Social Preferences

================================================================================
ABSTRACT
================================================================================

This review examines the neural circuitry underlying social preferences.
We discuss brain regions involved in fairness judgments, trust decisions,
and altruistic punishment. Understanding the neural basis of social
behavior provides new insights for economic theory.

================================================================================
KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. FAIRNESS CIRCUITS
   - Anterior insula for unfairness
   - Prefrontal cortex for deliberation
   - Automatic vs controlled

2. TRUST NETWORKS
   - Reward system involvement
   - Oxytocin effects
   - Betrayal detection

3. PUNISHMENT PROCESSING
   - Striatum activation
   - Satisfying nature of punishment
   - Costly but rewarding
