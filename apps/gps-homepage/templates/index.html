What is the GPS? The Global Preference Survey is a globally representative dataset on economic preferences, collected as part of the 2012 Gallup World Poll. It measures six fundamental preferences that drive human economic and social behavior: patience, risk taking, positive and negative reciprocity, altruism, and trust.
The Six Preference Dimensions

Patience

Willingness to delay gratification in exchange for future rewards. Measures time preference and intertemporal choice.

R² = 0.52 | GDP corr. = +0.62

Risk Taking

General willingness to take risks. Captures risk preference across financial, health, and social domains.

R² = 0.47 | GDP corr. = -0.31

Positive Reciprocity

Tendency to reward kind actions. Measures willingness to return favors and respond to generosity.

R² = 0.28 | GDP corr. = +0.15

Negative Reciprocity

Tendency to punish unkind actions. Measures willingness to engage in costly punishment of norm violations.

R² = 0.30 | GDP corr. = -0.08

Altruism

Unconditional willingness to give to others without expectation of direct return.

R² = 0.41 | GDP corr. = +0.42

Trust

Willingness to assume that other people have good intentions. Foundational for economic cooperation.

R² = 0.21 | GDP corr. = +0.54
Key Findings
Within-country heterogeneity dominates. Between-country variation accounts for only 6–12% of total variation in preferences, while 88–94% is within-country variation. This means individual differences matter far more than national averages — consistent with the EBF principle that context transforms parameters.
EBF Framework Integration
GPS ↔ EBF Mapping: GPS preference dimensions map to EBF Ψ-context dimensions and 10C CORE questions. Patience maps to ΨT (temporal context), risk taking to ΨC (cognitive context), and social preferences (reciprocity, altruism, trust) to ΨS (social context). The GPS provides anchor parameters (θA) for the Parameter Context Transformation (PCT).

About the Global Preference Survey
Citation: Falk, A., Becker, A., Dohmen, T., Enke, B., Huffman, D., & Sunde, U. (2018). Global Evidence on Economic Preferences. Quarterly Journal of Economics, 133(4), 1645–1692. DOI: 10.1093/qje/qjy013

Survey Methodology

The GPS data were collected as part of the 2012 Gallup World Poll, a survey that covers representative population samples in a large number of countries. The preference survey module consists of 12 question items measuring six economic preferences. The module was experimentally validated: survey responses significantly predict incentivized choice behavior.

Each preference is measured using a combination of quantitative and qualitative survey items. For instance, patience is measured via a hypothetical intertemporal choice (quantitative staircase) and a self-assessment of patience on a 0–10 scale (qualitative).

Coverage

The 76 countries in the GPS span all inhabited continents: 15 from the Americas, 25 from Europe, 22 from Asia and Pacific, and 14 from Africa (11 Sub-Saharan). Together they cover approximately 90% of the world population and global income. Approximately 1,000 respondents were surveyed per country, totaling about 80,000 individuals.

EBF Integration

In the Evidence-Based Framework (EBF), GPS data serve as anchor parameters for the Parameter Context Transformation (PCT). The key insight is that preference parameters are not fixed constants but functions of context: θ = f(Ψ, 10C).

The GPS provides country-level baselines that can be transformed to specific contexts using the PCT equation: θB = θA × ∏i M(ΔΨi).
